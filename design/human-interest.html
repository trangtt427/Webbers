Designing to Make 401(k)s Accessible for People in All Lines of Work

Human Interest is a modern, full-service 401(k) provider for small and mid-sized businesses that is on a mission to close the retirement gap in America. As a senior product designer, I'm embedded in a few top company initiatives, contributing to research, planning, and design execution. I often lend my efforts to the knowledge-building of current systems, painting the bigger picture for teams to get clarity, and then thinking creatively to envision an ideal state.

Platform and Internal Tools

My first projects at Human Interest involved building proprietary tools to help the business scale and cover gaps that were left as a result of a huge migration. I supported the Ops teams and probed into product and process inefficiencies. The theme for this area was automation and the goal was to bring that into our solutions and save teams from redundant, manual work. One of my fondest memories was when I collaborated with the Recordkeeping team to enable a service for deconverting plans, a critical business operation we've never been able to do before (my heart palpitations were off the charts seconds before launching this ðŸ¤ª).

Admin and Participant Experience

I lead design for one of our most business-transforming initiatives—self-serve onboarding for admins, a project at the intersection of revenue, partnerships, and automation. I'm currently designing to guide users through high stakes decision-making with a pleasant, intuitive flow. For the Participant Experience, I design around business goals like increasing participation rates and reducing cost-to-serve, while bringing value to participants through improved quality of life features in their app.

Relentlessly Pushing for Design Excellence at Human Interest

Outside of my product design responsibilities, I'm a design evangelist of sorts. I've worked alongside Leadership to develop core Design competencies to execute at the highest level of craft and to align on how we hire designers. I often engage with others to get a feel for how they perceive Design, and see where and how I could close knowledge gaps.

Due to privacy reasons, I can't show much of the work I've done. If you'd like to hear more about what I've been up to at Human Interest, feel free to reach out.
