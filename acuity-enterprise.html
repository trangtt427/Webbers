Acuity Enterprise

Scheduling Solutions for Squarespace Users

Designed an enterprise-grade Squarespace product that allows our largest customers to manage scheduling across their entire organizations.

Business Problem

Squarespace acquired Acuity and integrated the B2C offering into their ecosystem, but not the enterprise version, leaving our largest customers with a hugely fragmented product experience and lack of basic management and security features.

This meant that many of the user management workflows and settings lived in different places, depending on which version of the product was being used. For the B2C version of Acuity, they had the benefit of using Squarespace's identity services to manage users, while Acuity Enterprise had a separate risk-prone process of doing the same.

As the Design Lead, my role involved discovery of the competitive landscape and current processes, concepting, detailed design, creating a research plan for usability testing, scaling our design system, testing, and supporting throughout development.

Strategy

There were 5 areas that would be impacted with this migration.

Must be considered a "startup"

This is the first time a 401(k) plan would be offered at the company.

Must be a single employer

The company can't have any parent or children companies.

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider

The customer has to already be using our partner payroll provider.

Our target outcomes were to enable an end-to-end self-serve setup experience from our partner payroll provider's marketplace and establish a good start-to-complete rate for admins signing up.

Concepting

After facilitating a whiteboarding session with the CXO and Product Marketing Director, we landed on a vision of a "just click next" experience.

When designing a 401(k) plan, an admin can customize almost anything, but years of industry trends and insights suggested a few standard configurations that plans tend to lean towards. We aimed to offer curated plans based on those conventions to limit choice paralysis.

Usability Testing

Ideas were tested with six admin recruits to see if they can confidently complete all steps, including mock signing their plan docsâ€”the final action needed to launch their 401(k).

Some of our big questions:

Were terms and concepts easy to understand?
Where might admins get stuck in the flow?
How confident are admins in signing their plan docs?

Synthesized insights.

While users thought that the flow was intuitive and easy to follow, there were a few threats to our start-to-complete rate.

  • Safe Harbor was mysterious.

    Some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term. Participants said they'd Google it, which opens risk for abandonment.

  • Admins wanted to know what options were available to them.

    Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding them.

  • Admins felt that the most premium package was forced upon them.

    Users were confused by how the pricing page was laid out and felt that we were trying too hard to upsell.

mid-research update #1

I presented alternative designs to stop headlining with Safe Harbor.

We rapidly adapted designs in between participants and found that people were much more forgiving when Safe Harbor was subtly introduced with helpful context.

mid-research update #2

We also learned that the admin isn't always the authorized signer.

This meant that plan docs would need to be sent to someone else, altering the UI whether a signature is pending or not. We updated our flows to consider these complex scenarios.

Impact

Fast Track 401(k) was first released in beta to customers using our partner payroll provider and then to everyone else in September 2023. In the first week of its public release, we saw an increase of 900% signups!

Human Interest achieved preferred partner status and hit a recordbreaking amount of signups, validating that there is a market of savvy but busy admins who prefer self-serve. Another result of this successful project was that the company created a whole business group to support Fast Track 401(k). Read more about the product release here.
