Acuity Scheduling

Leading design 0-1 to uncover scheduling needs of our largest customers and deliver solutions to help manage and scale their appointments.

Role

    Led all discovery efforts (generative research + gap analyses); end-to-end design execution and evaluation (prototyping + moderated user testing); visual QA

Results

  • Closed 3 deals with healthcare and retail customers

  • Increased year over year customer retention

  • Enabled single sign-on for enterprise customers

Background

Acuity didn't fully integrate into the Squarespace ecosystem after the acquisition, leaving its largest customers with a severely fragmented product that lacked basic user management and security features.

Users who manage Acuity for their business, Org Managers, suffer the most with this gap. They are forced to create passwords and unsafely share them in order to onboard users to their orgs.

Account managers who support our Enterprise customers spend a ridiculous amount of time duplicating instances for customers to scale for multiple locations, because customers can't do it themselves in the product.

As the Lead Designer on the Enterprise triad, I led generative research into understanding our growing base of users, created design solutions and evaluated them with customers and lookalikes, and supported the team through all the UX changes that took place when implementing Squarespace code and technologies.

Our business goal was to deliver an enterprise product that helps customers scale their business, close contracts, and is unified with the rest of the Squarespace experience.

Solutions

I helped launch a flurry of improvements to the Enterprise experience, and a newly designed dashboard that housed it all.

The features I designed for the new Enterprise experience included:

A self-serve user invite and management experience

Several new features to support multi-location scheduling and organization-level management

The Enterprise Dashboard, a product exclusive to Organization Managers to oversee their scheduling operations

SSO flows (this one helped close a few deals that year)
