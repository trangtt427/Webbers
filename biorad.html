Bio‑Rad Microsite

The Challenge

Building a New Experience

For the first time in all of Bio‑Rad's existence, the company began focusing on accessibility beyond desktop. Bio‑Rad focused their efforts into building their first responsive microsite to showcase their top systems from the Clinical Diagnostics Group (CDG)and Life Sciences Group (LSG). This was a huge cross-functional effort with global buy-in from the executives.

My Role

Designer and Developer

I am part of a web team of three people within the MarCom department— the only team within the company to be working towards the responsive website. I'm responsible for:

  • CSS template
  • Visual design
  • Management and documentation from design to development
  • Wireframes and mock-ups
  • Iterating during sprints
  • Collaborating with other departments

Design Guide

When I came onto the project, several pages were already built but without emphasizing the mobile experience for users. The cause for this was due to lack of process and constant changes in requirements and deadlines. Our team inherited an antiquated design system from the Global Marketing Team in which we dismantled and created our own brand guide in order to accommodate web and mobile.

While the microsite would assume its own brand style, we stayed within the boundaries to make sure that it was easily recognizable with the Bio‑Rad brand.

Sitemap

Taxonomy

The tricky thing about this microsite is that it's meant to support global operations. While everyone can access our online, only some regions will see certain products. We created two branches for the website; one for the US and one for outside the US (OUS).

The working taxonomy

Working with Creative Copy

Product Messaging

I worked closely with the internal copywriter to place the appropriate content on our pages. Once that version is done, we use ProofHQ to share it with the marketing team to get their copy edits. The approved iteration then goes to Regulatory.

Sample ProofHQ version

Implementation

Launching on Marketo

We build our code on the local server and then host it on Marketo where we can link up logic for marketing leads. There are technical limitations working within Marketo due to their poor development environment. To combat this, our microsite was developed with a few hacks to overwrite some of Marketo's baked-in functions. The microsite was launched in sections to support Marketing's deadlines.

Development

Built website and launched internally

Validation

Tested microsite on major browsers (desktop and mobile)

Results

The microsite is continuing its course, but has changed a great deal within a few months. The overall feedback has been positive in terms of slowly introducing consistencies and patterns that unify the two departments. This project is a step in the right direction for the company towards their mission of providing better customer experience.

One of the biggest challenges for this project is the slow moving process that requires a lot of Regulatory input before implmentation. It is difficult to introduce industry standard techniques because the company has already adopted certain processes of their own which are outdated. However, there's a huge push to support what my team is doing and there's plenty of buy-in for it.
