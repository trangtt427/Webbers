Office Space IoT Web App

Big Data, IoT, Analytics, Website, Front-end Development, Visual Design

The Challenge

Refresh the UX

One of our products for an office space client is a web-based software that houses building automation controls. At the time of conception, Controlco didn't have any UX designers or any design guidelines at all, so products were engineering-focused. I was tasked with revamping the product to improve the experience.

The existing interface needed consistent designs, user-friendly interactions, recognizable design patterns, and an overall pleasant experience.

Current Issues

The current design presents a few outstanding issues:

  1. No proper homepage
  2. Font and colors are inconsistent on each page
  3. Dropdowns being used but only showing two choices
  4. Poor use of lightbox

From my inquiries with some of our users, it was apparent that they didn't want me to move their data around. They were used to how things were laid out and were concerned about re-learning the flow. I also learned that they wanted a way to see an overview of their systems upon logging in. I translated this requirement into a homepage that will show relevant KPIs. I also made it clear that I was going to make the interface intuitive so that they wouldn't have to worry about learnability.

Wireframing

Wireframe depicting the architecture and flow of the new layout

Users log in and are greeted by KPIs of each system: HVAC, lighting, alarms, and central plant. Users will see which system needs their attention by looking at how the systems are performing on the homepage.

I was maintaining communication with the team of data modelers throughout the entire process. It was a highly iterative process that required a lot of redrafting of element placements, especially because of new equipment installations onsite.

Style Guide

Development began with the backend functionalities while I was finishing the final phase of the design. The design artifacts were handed over to the engineering team to complete the implementation.

Evaluating the Usability

I ran a few guerilla-styled usability tests with some of the data modelers via remote screensharing. I recruited a total of six data modelers to carry out the same tasks. Three of them used the old interface while the other three used the new interface. The tasks include:

  1. Report how many units are occupied
  2. Add the followng event to August 1, 2017: CL2, event named "cats", event class "Override", start time is "this time", end time mode is "sunrise", and end time is "2:30pm", end date is "August 9, 2017"

For the old interface, the average time to complete the two tasks, respectively, were 2.44 seconds and 25.32 seconds. For the new interface, the average times were 1.02 seconds and 20.21 seconds. Although by a small margin, users are able to complete their common tasks faster with the newly designed interface. These metrics supported the new design as being more favorable.

The Takeaway

Controlco's New Look

"I like the new look. It kind of looks like Apple's stuff. It also reminds me of an app that my 4-year old kid plays on her iPad."
- Data Modeler

The final product was warmly received by all stakeholders (VP, engineering, and data modelers). One data modeler even said that the new interface was easy to use and reminded him of his daughter's iPad game. I translated that to "easy enough that a 4-year old can use it". The client ended up using this design as the standard for all enterprise level projects in their portfolio. It was a step in the right direction and a new mentality towards better usability.
