Bio-Rad Microsite

The Challenge

Building a New Experience

For the first time in all of Bio‑Rad's existence, the company pivot focus towards supporting interactions beyond desktop. Bio‑Rad began the initiative to build their first responsive microsite ever which shows all products from the Clinical Diagnostics Group (CDG)and Life Sciences Group (LSG). This was a huge collaborative effort with great buy-in from the company and was slated to take over two years.

My Role

MarCom Web Team

I was part of the unique Web Team within the MarCom department— the only team within the company to be working towards the microsite and its responsive components. I was responsible for:

  • CSS template
  • Visual design
  • Exploring design and dev trends
  • Management and documentation from design to development
  • Wireframing and mock-ups
  • Establish best practices across departments

Design Guidelines

When I came onto the project, several pages were already built but without any nod to user experience. The cause for this was due to lack of process and constant changes to requirements and deadlines. One of the first and most important things I did was advocate for better UX trends in our layouts. This involves cleaning up the CSS and implementing consistencies in each section. My team inherited an antiquated design system from the Global Marketing Team in which we dismantled and created our own brand guidelines in order to accommodate the microsite.

The new template that was established by our team

Documentation on how to build our containers for the microsite

While the microsite would assume its own brand style, I made sure we stayed within the boundaries to make sure that it was easily recognizable with the Bio‑Rad brand.

Sitemap

Taxonomy

The tricky thing about this microsite is that it's meant to support global operations. While anyone can access our online catalogue, not everyone will have access to certain products in their country. We have two branches for the microsite; one for the US and one for outside the US (OUS). The products and messaging are slightly different between the two versions.

Working with Creative Copy

Product Messaging

I worked closely with the internal copywriter to place the appropriate content on our pages. Once that version is done, we use ProofHQ to share it with the marketing team. After that iteration is approved, it goes to Regulatory. During the periods while we wait on copy edits, we are fixing bugs, altering designs, and testing.

Iterations are shown on ProofHQ to gather edit requests from stakeholders

Process Overview

Defining the problem

Design and develop a microsite for a company that has not incorporated modern trends in user experience

Gathering information

Spoke to Marketing and Regulatory to determine copy and content structure

Present Solutions

Made edits to copy and collaborated using ProofHQ

Visual design

Worked on bug fixes and upkeep of CSS

Introduced new ideas into layout and design guide

Development

Built website and launched internally

Validation

Did litmus test and sent out links internally to test out microsite

Results

The microsite is continuing on its course, but has changed a great deal within a few months. The overall feedback has been positive in terms of slowly introducing consistencies and patterns that unify the two departments.

The company of Bio-Rad is looking to my team for pushing the limits of customer experience. The biggest challenge is trying to get buy-in for industry-standard processes.
