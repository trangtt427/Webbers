opportunity

Making 401(k)s Accessible for People in All Lines of Work

In 2015, Human Interest (known as Captain401 at the time) embarked on building their own recordkeeper to manage all plans in-house. A recordkeeper is responsible for keeping track of plan assets and handling almost all money movement. Usually the big incumbents have in-house recordkeepers—other smaller 401(k) providers use a third-party.

Human Interest was also using a third-party, but was ready to bring all of their plans onto their own recordkeeping platform so that they could provide a better end-to-end 401(k) experience for customers without having to pay a premium. We needed to make sure that we could stand on our own two feet first before saying bye to the third-party. As the lead designer on this front, I help create products and tools needed to support this herculean initiative, while ensuring a 401(k) experience that is uncomplicated and optimal for users.

I also lead design for complex but mission-critical initiatives spanning the Administrator, Internal, and some of the Participant Experiences. I work to ensure that all users, internal or external, are able to complete their tasks through a frictionless experience. I can't disclose any of this work for privacy reasons, but feel free to reach out if you'd like to hear more.
