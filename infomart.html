Infomart Energy Dashboard

The Challenge

Designing for IoT

While Controlco was in the midst of building out the software for a few Infomart data centers, we didn't have a feature to show the summary of powers. As my first large scale autonomous project at Controlco, I was responsible for creating this part of the product. The feature would be a dashboard for users to track their energy for all meters.

This dashboard would be a read-only deliverable where users will not be able to change operational settings. The goal was to create an interface that showed onsite technicians what they wanted, but without overwhelming them with too many tables of IoT data.

Real Power vs. Apparent Power

Users are used to seeing both real power and apparent power, kW and kVA, for every point. However, they also sectioned these points into three phases, which meant there would be hundreds of points to show. The engineers claimed that they needed all of these points present at first glance, but I felt that I could come up with a solution to keep it usable and less cluttered.

Design

Since I had the opportunity for exploration, I came up with the idea to include a toggle between the two forms of energy.

The data center contains a few power units: power distribution units (PDU), uninterruptible power supplies (UPS), and distribution panels. There are several of each unit, and they also contain phases of electrical power (A,B,C). The users wanted to see a live average as well as a total sum of all units. I met these requirements but also spruced the interface from the previous energy summary pages in the following ways:

  • Different styles for every other row for better usability
  • Sum is much more apparent and appears on the right of each table
  • Toggle that changes logic to show different forms of energy

Process Overview

Define the problem

"How do I help users find data more easily?"

Gather information

Reviewed past energy dashboards and spoke to data center engineers

Present possible solutions

Showed wireframe of table concept showing toggle between real and apparent power

Visual design

Came up with color palette and type

Implementation

Built directly on DGLux5 platform

Evaluate

Allowed users access and received feedback that overall design made it easier to find numbers

The Takeaway

Cleaner Data

Sometimes customers will have a grand idea without thinking too much into the technicality of it. As a designer, it was my job to communicate to the clients and figure out what they wanted. In this case, I had enough buy-in to alter the presentation of data. The users had no problem using a toggle to show the units of power separately. The new design and feature were implemented in the rest of the Infomart data centers that we've integrated.
