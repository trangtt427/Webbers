Vudu Website

The Challenge

Improving Vudu's Web UI

After spending much of my time watching shows and movies via Netflix, HBO, and Hulu, I found the interface of Vudu a little less friendly. Upon using Vudu for the first time, I was greeted with lots of titles and ads fighting for my attention. This gave me the idea to come up with a design for their web interface through a passion project.

Gathering User Information

Creating a Persona

Based on the feedback of several users of streaming services including Vudu, I was able to identify a common motive. People wanted to watch something that has been promoted via word of mouth or with great reviews. I created a fictitious customer to help me figure out my priorities.

Pibby Pibberson
  • Gender: Male
  • Age: 38
  • Occupation: Sales Development Representative
  • Marital Status: Married
  • User Goals: Spend less time looking for movies, wants super easy to use interface, and access to action movies in fewest amount of clicks
  • Motivations: Time and usability
  • Personality Traits: Hard-working, hates thinking too hard, hates wasting time
  • Personal Devices: Macbook Pro 2016, iPhone X

I sketched out a storyboard in comic-panel style to help me visualize the interactions. This also highlights the emotions that users might have as they carry on through the website.

Wireframing

Some of the features that I've implemented include:

  • Optimized carousel that use high resolution images to promote titles
  • Inline CTAs that let users make decisions without a lot of nested options
  • Snail trail for better focus on a few titles at a time
  • Readily available synopses so users won't have to delve past two layers to learn about a title

The New Interface

The Takeaway

A Simple Project Goes a Long Way

This project presented an opportunity for me to take a known household name and put my own flair on it, working within limitations of the brand. Although it was a passion project, I still had to apply traditional design methods and worked with people to obtain information, even though it was very casual. The skills and methods I've used here have applied to subsequent professional projects as well.
