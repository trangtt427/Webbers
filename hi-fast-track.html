Human Interest

Introducing Fast Track 401(k), the industry's first self-serve onboarding to help Americans achieve financial freedom.

Role

Design Lead

Results

  • Human Interest became a preferred partner to a top small business payroll provider
  • Increased 401(k) signups within a week of product release

Business Problem

Admins go through the frustrating process of setting up a Human Interest 401(k) plan online only to find out that they can't launch their plan without talking to a specialist.

This was inefficient and infuriating for admins coming from our partner payroll provider, who just want to do things asynchronously without needing human intervention.

The onboarding UI at the time, which saw little change since the company's inception.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

The entire setup process was painful for admins.

  • The interface overwhelmed users with unclear steps, inconsistent patterns, and loads of industry jargon.
  • Admins were being asked to make important decisions, but there wasn't enough helpful context in the app.
  • Even if admins toughed out the online setup, they were forced to meet with a specialist (or a few) to get their plan started, regardless of how simple their plan was.

While there are some admins who get stuck and need customer support, Human Interest recognized that there are others who prefer to do everything themselves.

One prospect took the time to share the following feedback with our CRO.

"You guys have what feels to me like an overly aggressive and heavy onboarding/sales team.This is the second time your team has tried to get me on a 45 minute call. There are now three support people from your side to help set up one account with a client who has zero issue (and in fact prefers) all async."

Objective

We needed to offer a self-serve onboarding experience to our customers by June in order to be in good standing with one of the country's top payroll providers.

There wasn't enough time to make self-serve happen for all customers, so we focused our efforts designing for companies that fulfill the following criteria.

Must be considered a "startup"
This is the first time a 401(k) plan would be offered at the company.
Must be a single employer

This means that the company can't have any parent or children companies.

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider
The customer has to already be using our partner payroll provider.

Our target outcomes were to allow an end-to-end self-service signup experience from our partner payroll provider's marketplace and to establish a good start-to-complete rate.

Concepting

After facilitating a 2.5 hours-long whiteboarding session with the CXO and Product Marketing Director, we landed on a vision of a "just click next" experience.

When designing their 401(k) plan, admins can choose options for eligibility, employer match, vesting, and length of service. However, years of industry trends and our own data suggested a few standard configurations that companies lean towards. We aimed to offer curated plans based on those conventions to limit choice paralysis. This new experience includes social proof, in-app education, and reduced industry jargon.

We jammed and riffed off of each other, while comparing our ideas to solutions in the wild.

Initial sketches were cleaned up and translated into lofi wireframes.

The new designs radically simplified the setup flow.

We only ask admins a few choices about their plan design, thanks to integrations with our payroll partner that already pull in the admin's data.

Usability Testing

Ideas were tested with six admin recruits to see if they can confidently complete all steps, including mock signing their plan docs—the final action needed to kick off their 401(k) plan.

I created the research plan, guided by the following questions:

Were terms and concepts easy to understand?
Where might admins get stuck in the flow?
How confident are admins in signing the plan docs?

Synthesized insights for each of the flows.

While users thought that the flow was intuitive and easy to follow, there were a few threats to our start-to-complete rate.

  • Safe Harbor was mysterious.

    Some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term. Participants said they'd Google it, which opens risk for abandonment.

  • Admins didn't know what all the available options were.

    Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding the non-premium ones.

  • Admins felt that they were "forced" towards a certain package.

    Users were confused by how the pricing page was laid out and felt that we were trying too hard to upsell.

improvement

I presented alternative designs to stop headlining with "Safe Harbor".

We rapidly iterated on designs in between participants, and found that people were much more forgiving with Safe Harbor sprinkled in with context.

improvement

We also learned that the admin sometimes isn't the authorized signer.

This meant that plan docs would need to be sent to someone else, outside of the onboarding flow. We iterated on the designs to consider these complex scenarios.

Final Designs

In collaboration with the front end Engineering team, we evolved the current design system to support the new product surfaces as I updated the prototypes to their detailed design forms.

Onboarding screens with detailed visual design applied.

Impact

Fast Track 401(k) was first made available in a beta release to customers using our partner payroll provider and finally to everyone else in September 2023.

Human Interest achieved preferred partner status and in the week the product was launched, at least ten people signed up via Fast Track 401(k), proving that people would rather skip talking to a human if they have the choice. Read more about the product announcement here.
