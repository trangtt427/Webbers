Fast Track 401(k) by Human Interest gives businesses the confidence to launch their first retirement plan. I designed the setup experience to be simple enough to be completed it in just minutes.

Results

  • 900% increase in 401(k) plan signups

  • Helped company remain in good standing as preferred 401(k) partner

Business Problem

An overly difficult onboarding process put the company on the brink of losing huge margins.

After receiving customer complaints about the Human Interest setup experience, a top SMB payroll provider asked us to get to the bottom of things. This was detrimental because referrals from partner payroll providers make up a big slice of our signups.

The onboarding UI at the time, which saw little change since the company's humble beginnings.

The onboarding UI at the time, which saw little change since the company's humble beginnings.

The current setup flow threw daunting jargon at admins without clear definitions and didn't clue them in to where they're at in terms of progress. Also, admins are forced to speak to a Human Interest onboarding specialist in order to launch their 401(k).

We needed to offer a better self-serve experience within six months, or be removed from our partner's list of preferred 401(k) providers.

This became the company's highest priority for the year, touching domains across growth, sales, integrations, product, and legal. My role involved (1) helping the team understand admin behaviors with secondary research and interviewing onboarding specialists, (2) carrying designs from concept to detailed, (3) scaling the design system, and (4) a bit of quality assurance.

Strategy

In order to meet the deadline in six months, we focused on making self-serve happen only for the most common plan types.

Must be a "startup" plan—a plan offered for the first time to the company's participants

Must be a single employer—the company can't have any parent or children companies

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider

Concepting

I led a 2-hour long whiteboarding session with the Chief Experience Officer and Product to land on a "just click next" concept that offers curated plans to limit choice paralysis.

When designing a 401(k) plan, admins can customize almost anything, but industry trends suggest a few popular configurations.

Sketches showing areas that require explicit decision-making from admins.

Initial sketches were cleaned up and translated into lofi wireframes.

Using screening questions, we can route the qualified prospects to the appropriate experiences.

If admins answered "no" to any of these questions (which is uncommon), it would mean that their plans are too complex and would need to talk to a specialist.

The Enhanced Match is defaulted because it's one of the most common configurations in the industry.

We added social proof and in-app education to help admins who were newer to 401(k)s.

Admins are interested in plans that are safe and common amongst their industry peers. In this paradigm shift, we show popular plan designs along with plenty of helpful information at the right time.

The new designs radically simplified the setup flow. We only ask admins a few things about their plan, thanks to integrations with our payroll partner that already pull in admin data.

While users thought that the flow was simple, there were a few threats to our start-to-complete rate.

Safe Harbor was mysterious. Some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term. Participants said they'd Google it, which opens risk for abandonment.

Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding them. Admins felt that the most premium package was forced on them.

mid-research update #1

We tweaked the designs to stop headling with Safe Harbor and found that people were much more forgiving when it was subtly introduced in body text.

mid-research update #2

The trustee and the authorized signer are sometimes not the same people. I updated designs to include an experience in the case of pending signatures from different parties.

Impact

Fast Track 401(k) was first released in beta to customers using our partner payroll provider and then to everyone else in September 2023. In the first week of its public release, we saw an increase of 900% signups.

Human Interest achieved preferred partner status and hit a recordbreaking amount of signups, validating that there is a market of savvy but busy admins who prefer self-serve. Another result of this successful project was that the company created a whole business group to support Fast Track 401(k). Read more about the product release here.
