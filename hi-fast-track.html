Human Interest

Introducing Fast Track 401(k), the industry's first self-serve onboarding to help Americans achieve financial freedom.

Role

Design Lead

Results

  • Human Interest became a preferred partner to a top small business payroll provider
  • Increased 401(k) signups within a week of product release

Business Problem

Admins endure a frustrating process of setting up a Human Interest 401(k) plan online only to find out that they can't finish without talking to a specialist.

This wasn't the best use of Human Interest resources and was infuriating for admins coming from our partner payroll provider, who just want to do things without needing human intervention.

The onboarding UI at the time, which saw little change since the company's inception.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

The signup experience was painful for admins.

  • Users were overwhelmed by unclear steps, inconsistent patterns, and industry jargon in the UI.
  • Users were being asked to make important decisions without enough context.
  • Users who toughed out the online setup were forced to meet with a specialist (or a few) to get their plan started, even if their plans were simple.

One of our partners, a top payroll provider, raised this issue and demanded that we offer a better onboarding experience in order to remain in good standing with them.

Strategy

There wasn't enough time to make self-serve happen for all types of customers, so we focused on non-complex plans that fulfill the following criteria.

Must be considered a "startup"
This is the first time a 401(k) plan would be offered at the company.
Must be a single employer

This means that the company can't have any parent or children companies.

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider
The customer has to already be using our partner payroll provider.

Our target outcomes were to (1) enable an end-to-end self-serve setup experience from our partner payroll provider's marketplace and (2) establish a good start-to-complete rate for admins signing up.

Concepting

After facilitating a whiteboarding session with the CXO and Product Marketing Director, we landed on a vision of a "just click next" experience.

When designing a 401(k) plan, an admin can customize almost anything, but years of industry trends and insights suggested a few standard configurations that companies lean towards. We aimed to offer curated plans based on those conventions to limit choice paralysis. This paradigm shift includes social proof, in-app education, and reduced jargon.

We jammed for hours and compared our ideas to solutions in the wild.

Usability Testing

Ideas were tested with six admin recruits to see if they can confidently complete all steps, including mock signing their plan docsâ€”the final action needed to kick off their 401(k) plan.

I created the research plan, guided by the following questions:

Were terms and concepts easy to understand?
Where might admins get stuck in the flow?
How confident are admins in signing the plan docs?

Synthesized insights.

While users thought that the flow was intuitive and easy to follow, there were a few threats to our start-to-complete rate.

  • Safe Harbor was mysterious.

    Some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term. Participants said they'd Google it, which opens risk for abandonment.

  • Admins didn't know what all the available options were.

    Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding them.

  • Admins felt "forced" towards a certain package.

    Users were confused by how the pricing page was laid out and felt that we were trying too hard to upsell.

improvement

I presented alternative designs to stop headlining with Safe Harbor.

We rapidly iterated on designs in between participants, and found that people were much more forgiving with Safe Harbor sprinkled in with context.

improvement

We also learned that the admin isn't always the authorized signer.

This meant that plan docs would need to be sent to someone else to sign. We updated our flows to consider these complex scenarios.

Impact

Fast Track 401(k) was first released in beta to customers using our partner payroll provider and then to everyone else in September 2023.

Human Interest achieved preferred partner status and in the week the product was launched, at least ten people signed up via Fast Track 401(k), proving that there is a market of competent admins who would rather skip talking to a human. Read more about the product announcement here.
