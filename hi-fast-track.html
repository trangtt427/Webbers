Human Interest

Introducing Fast Track 401(k), the industry's first self-serve onboarding to help Americans achieve financial freedom.

Role

Design Lead

Results

  • Human Interest became a preferred partner to a top small business payroll provider
  • Increased 401(k) signups within a week of product release

business problem

Admins go through the process of setting up a Human Interest 401(k) plan online only to find out that they can't complete it without talking to a specialist.

This process was expensive for Human Interest and frustrating for admins referred by our partner payroll provider, whose product experience is considered the gold-standard in the industry.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

The setup experience was painful for admins.

  • Admins were overwhelmed and confused by the complicated steps and inconsistent progress patterns in the flow.
  • Important decisions were being asked of admins, but there wasn't enough guidance on how to decide.
  • There was more industry jargon than what users were comfortable with.
  • Even though admins finished the online flow, they were required to meet with a specialist to start their 401(k).

objective

We needed to offer a self-serve onboarding experience to our customers by June in order to be in good standing with one of the country's top payroll providers.

There wasn't enough time to make self-serve happen for all customers, so we focused our efforts designing for companies that satisfy all of the following requirements.

Must be considered a "startup"
This is the first time a 401(k) plan would be offered at the company.
Must be a single employer

This means that the company can't have any parent or children companies.

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider
The customer has to already be using our partner payroll provider.

Our indicator of success for this initiative is to establish a start-to-complete rate.

concepting

After facilitating a 2.5 hours-long whiteboarding session with the CXO and Product Marketing Director, we landed on a vision of a "just click next" experience.

When designing their 401(k) plan, admins can choose options for eligibility, employer match, vesting, and length of service. However, years of industry trends and our own data suggested a few standard configurations that companies lean towards. We aimed to offer curated plans based on those conventions to limit choice paralysis. This new experience includes social proof, in-app education, and reduced industry jargon.

We jammed and riffed off of each other, while comparing our ideas to solutions in the wild.

Initial sketches were cleaned up and translated into lofi wireframes.

The new designs radically simplified the setup flow.

We only ask Admins a few choices about their plan design, thanks to integrations with our payroll partner to pull in the admin's data.

usability testing

We put our ideas to the test with six admin recruits to see if they can confidently complete all steps, including mock signing their plan docs.

Our research plan was guided by the following questions:

Were all the terms and concepts easy to understand?
Where might admins get stuck in the flow?
How confident are admins in signing the plan docs?

Synthesized insights for each of the flows.

While users thought that the flow was intuitive and easy to follow, there were a few threats to our start-to-complete rate.

  • Safe Harbor was mysterious.

    Some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term.

  • Admins didn't know what all the available options were.

    Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding the non-premium ones.

  • Pricing was confusing.

    Users felt they were being pidgeonholed to a price point.

improvement

I presented alternative designs to stop headlining with "Safe Harbor".

We rapidly iterated on designs in between participants, and found that people were much more forgiving with Safe Harbor sprinkled in with context.

improvement

We also learned that the admin sometimes isn't the authorized signer.

This meant that plan docs would need to get sent to someone else, outside of the onboarding flow. We iterated on the designs to consider these complex scenarios.

final designs

In collaboration with our front end Engineering team, we evolved our current design system to support the new product surfaces as I moved the prototypes to their detailed design forms.

Onboarding screens with detailed visual design applied.

impact

Fast Track 401(k) was first made available to customers using our partner payroll provider, and then to everyone else in September 2023.

Human Interest achieved preferred partner status and in the week the product was launched, we saw at least ten people sign up, proving that people would rather skip talking to a human if they have the choice. Read more about the product announcement announcement here.
