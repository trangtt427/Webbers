Human Interest

I led design for Fast Track 401(k), the industry's first self-guided onboarding to help admins set up their company's 401(k). This resulted in an influx of signups and a deepened partnership with a top payroll provider for SMBs.

Business Problem

When signing up for a 401(k) online with Human Interest, admins go through a grueling process nuanced with jargon and unnecessary calls with onboarding specialists.

The onboarding UI at the time, which saw little change since the company's inception.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

    Core Issues

  • Not enough context to help admins understand industry terms and how they affect tax and legal obligations.
  • Inconsistent UI patterns and conflicting messaging around what admins have to do and what they should expect next.
  • Process is pitched as self-guided, but by the end, confident admins who've completed setup can only launch their plans by talking to a specialist.

Human Interest had put all efforts to improve this experience on the back burner until one of our partners—a top payroll provider responsible for a lot of our leads—demanded a better signup experience. To remain in good standing with them, we had to deliver this within four months. This initiative became the company's highest priority for the first half of the year, touching systems across growth, sales, integrations, internal tooling, product, and legal.

As the Design Lead, my roles involved building understanding of the competitive landscape, concepting, detailed design, creating a research plan for usability testing, scaling our design system, and QA (visual and functional).

Strategy

To make self-serve happen by our partner's given timeline, we focused on non-complex plans that fulfill the following criteria.

Must be considered a "startup"

This is the first time a 401(k) plan would be offered at the company.

Must be a single employer

This means that the company can't have any parent or children companies.

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider

The customer has to already be using our partner payroll provider.

Our target outcomes were to (1) enable an end-to-end self-serve setup experience from our partner payroll provider's marketplace and (2) establish a good start-to-complete rate for admins signing up.

Concepting

After facilitating a whiteboarding session with the CXO and Product Marketing Director, we landed on a vision of a "just click next" experience.

When designing a 401(k) plan, an admin can customize almost anything, but years of industry trends and insights suggested a few standard configurations that plans tend to lean towards. We aimed to offer curated plans based on those conventions to limit choice paralysis.

Usability Testing

Ideas were tested with six admin recruits to see if they can confidently complete all steps, including mock signing their plan docs—the final action needed to launch their plan.

I created the research plan, guided by the following questions:

Were terms and concepts easy to understand?
Where might admins get stuck in the flow?
How confident are admins in signing the plan docs?

Synthesized insights.

While users thought that the flow was intuitive and easy to follow, there were a few threats to our start-to-complete rate.

  • Safe Harbor was mysterious.

    Some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term. Participants said they'd Google it, which opens risk for abandonment.

  • Admins didn't know what all the available options were.

    Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding them.

  • Admins felt that the most premium package was forced upon them.

    Users were confused by how the pricing page was laid out and felt that we were trying too hard to upsell.

mid-research update #1

I presented alternative designs to stop headlining with Safe Harbor.

We rapidly adapted designs in between participants and found that people were much more forgiving when Safe Harbor was subtly introduced with helpful context.

mid-research update #2

We also learned that the admin isn't always the authorized signer.

This meant that plan docs would need to be sent to someone else, altering the UI whether a signature is pending or not. We updated our flows to consider these complex scenarios.

I supported the front end Engineering team in evolving the design system so that it could be scaled for the new product surfaces.

Impact

Fast Track 401(k) was first released in beta to customers using our partner payroll provider and then to everyone else in September 2023.

Human Interest achieved preferred partner status and in the week the product was launched, at least thirty people signed up via Fast Track 401(k), validating that there is a market of savvy but busy admins who prefer self-serve. Read more about the product release here.
