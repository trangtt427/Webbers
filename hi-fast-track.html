Human Interest

Introducing Fast Track 401(k), the industry's first self-serve onboarding to help Americans achieve financial freedom.

Role

Design Lead

Results

  • Human Interest became a Preferred Partner to one of the top SMB payroll providers
  • Increased admin signups within a week of the product releasing

business problem

Admins begin setting up a Human Interest 401(k) plan online only to find out that they can't proceed without talking to a specialist.

This process was expensive for Human Interest and frustrated admins referred by our partner payroll provider, whose product experience is considered the gold-standard in the industry.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

The current onboarding UI that remained unchanged since the company's inception.

The entire setup process was painful.

Admins were overwhelmed by the number of steps and confused by the inconsistent cues of their progress.

Important decisions were being asked of admins, but there wasn't enough guidance as to how to decide.

There was too much industry jargon that admins were uncomfortable with.

objective

We needed to offer a self-serve onboarding experience to our customers by June in order to be in good standing with one of the country's top payroll providers.

There wasn't enough time to make self-serve happen for all customers, so we focused our efforts designing for plans that satisfy all of the following requirements.

Must be considered a "startup"
This is the first time that their company is offering a 401(k) plan to their employees.
Must be a single employer

This means that the company can't have any parent or children companies.

Must be a referral from our partner payroll provider
The customer has to already be using our partner payroll provider

concepting

After facilitating a 2.5 hours-long whiteboarding session with the CXO and Product Marketing Director, we landed on a vision of a "just click next" experience.

We aimed to offer curated plans based on the standard configurations that we've seen in our data. This new experience includes social proof, in-app education, and no industry jargon.

We jammed and riffed off of each other, while comparing our ideas to solutions in the wild.

Initial sketches were cleaned up and translated into lofi wireframes.

The new designs aimed to radically simplify the setup flow.

We only ask Admins a few choices about their plan design, thanks to integrations with our partner to pull in the admin's profile and defaulted selections backed by data.

Synthesized insights for each of the flows.

The results: Safe Harbor is mysterious. Admins couldn't see their options. The pricing was confusing.

We fell short of effectively communicating what Safe Harbor was in our designs (some thought it was a company, others thought it was a Human Interest-coined term). Language in our flow suggested that there were different tiers of service, but users had trouble finding the route to non-premium options. I presented alternative designs to stop headlining with "Safe Harbor", ease the upsell of premium options, and apply familiar ecommerce patterns.

The new Service Package page performed better in testing.

Polished final designs.

Impact

After going live with a beta phase, the product was made available to everyone in September 2023. Read more about the release announcement here.

Human Interest successfully made it as a preferred partner. We saw at least ten people go through the flow the first week the product was launched, proving that people would rather skip talking to a human if they have the choice.
