Vudu For Web
Visual Design | UX Design | Desktop

Project Overview

After spending much of my time watching shows and movies via Netflix, HBO, and Hulu, I found the interface of Vudu a little less friendly. Upon using Vudu for the first time, I was greeted with lots of titles and ads fighting for my attention. This gave me the idea to come up with a design for their web interface.

Incorporating User Feedback

Based on the feedback of several users of streaming services including Vudu, I was able to identify a common motive. People wanted to watch something that has been promoted via word of mouth or with great reviews. I created a fictitious customer to help me figure out my priorities.

Pibby Pibberson
  • Gender: Male
  • Age: 38
  • Occupation: Sales Development Representative
  • Marital Status: Married
  • Bio: Pibby's job requires him to be persistent and extroverted, but he's always exhausted when he comes home every evening. He also endures a 1.5hr commute on the way home. Once home, Pibby enjoys a cold beverage and unwinding with his wife and five year old son. Usually, Pibby likes action movies and comedies, but will sometimes watch other genres with his wife. On the weekends, Pibby will have a family movie night with a movie title of his son's choice.
  • User Goals: Spend less time looking for movies, wants super easy to use interface, and access to action movies in fewest amount of clicks
  • Motivations: Time, speed, and price
  • Personality Traits: Hard-working, hates thinking too hard, hates wasting time
  • Personal Devices: Macbook Pro 2016, iPhone X

I sketched out a storyboard in comic-panel style to help me visualize the interactions. This also highlights the emotions that users might have as they carry on through the website.

User Task Flow

Along with the storyboard, I created a simple timeline that plays out as Pibby focuses on his goal. This flow helped me conceptualize features that should be implemented in order to improve the overall interaction.

Pibby goes to Vudu website

Under "Action", Pibby searches for a movie

Pibby clicks on the arrow that further expands the title, showing options to rent or buy

After some thought, Pibby decides not to go through with that title

The timed carousel changes and catches Pibby's attention

He clicks on the feature to open a synopsis along with some CTAs

Pibby makes the decision to rent the HD version of this film and watches it

Wireframing

Some of the features that I've implemented include:

  • Optimized carousel that use high resolution images to promote titles
  • Inline CTAs that let users make decisions without a lot of nested options
  • Snail trail for better focus on a few titles at a time
  • Readily available synopses so users won't have to delve past two layers to learn about a title

Most of the improvements involved re-organizing the information present

The New Interface

The Takeaway

Working on this project was a fun opportunity that let me design for customers who enjoy the same pleasure of streaming like myself. I haven't had the chance to do that for a while, since many of my projects are geared towards a more technical audience. I was limited on resources since this wasn't a professional project, but was happy enough to be able to get some feedback from some willing people.
