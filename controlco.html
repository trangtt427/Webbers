Controlco

Web Solution for Smart Buildings

Product design | user research | visual design

Since the company's inception, there had been no design thinking approach at Controlco. Engineers took requirements straight from the CEO to develop a product that lacked polish and a consistent user experience. In 2016, I was the first dedicated UX hire. I headed up a redesign initiative to bring technicians a revamped experience, while introducing industry-standard tools and a user-focused approach to product development.

opportunity

"It helps me get the job done in the most roundabout way ever."

Controlco integrates HVAC, lighting, energy, and other systems into a centralized network where all of that data is accessible and controlled from a UI. Unfortunately, features were piecemealed together without strategy and fully understanding user needs, resulting in a classic Frankenstein product. Information was cluttered and not prioritized, basic actions were missing, the visual design was unappealing— all of this made for a product experience that users complained about.

research and discoveries

Diving Into the Controlco User's Workflow

I met with three different facility technicians who work at the real estate firm that we were building for. The facility technicians (also called controls/BAS technicians) have a vast and blended knowledge of HVAC, lighting, mechanical, and systems programming. Their role is to keep facilities running in an optimal environment. I held sessions to understand their actions in the Controlco app, capture their sentiments and frustrations, and identify gaps for improvement.

User Story Mapping

I mapped out the steps users would take to accomplish some of their day-to-day tasks.

Insights showed that some parts of the users' flow were not intuitive at all.

  • Table actions like searching, sorting, and filtering were not available, making it incredibly difficult to sift through the information users needed.
  • Messaging was confusing for users (ex. unacknowledged and acknowledged alarms).
  • The only way to see statuses and active alarms for equipment was to manually go to each system's tab in the navigation.
  • There were too many interactions involved for a single task. Dropdowns were being used for inputs with only two options and users couldn't make bulk changes.
  • The experience was disruptive and inconsistent. Each page looked like it came from different apps and several fonts were being used.

Uncovering What Users Really Wanted

Our discoveries led us to believe that the platform lacked direction for users. Users felt that having a high level view of their system would help identify where they should be focusing their attention. Research revealed that users were interested in knowing:

  1. How many units are on/offline.
  2. What alarms were activated.
  3. Are there any statuses users should be aware of.

In addition to wanting a sense of guidance as they work, users shared their feelings of dread when interacting with the app. Despite working in an industry where design is only an afterthought, the facility technicians still criticized the app for not being intuitive nor engaging enough to make their assignments more pleasant.

Vision

Our goal was to redesign the Controlco web app to increase productivity through a delightful experience catered to users.

Criteria for Success

  1. Provide a way that can better inform users the overall health of their system and what they need to achieve.
  2. Improve the interaction patterns so they can handle their tasks quickly and efficiently.
  3. Use language that is concise and clear.
  4. Revamp each page of the core product to have a cohesive visual identity.

design

Conceptualizing a Homepage Dashboard

The Controlco platform was remiss in giving users a big picture view of their facility. I proposed a homepage dashboard of KPIs that would help users understand how their system was behaving. With this information prioritized, users would be to drill down into any equipment of interest for assessment.

At first, technicians said that they needed specific data points on the dashboard.

I picked the technicians' brains about what KPIs they wanted to see and how they'd be beneficial. I wanted to make sure that the dashboard was not convoluted with extraneous details and that we were showing data that would be most helpful to users. The tradeoff of including a lot of KPIs meant that the dashboard would lose its purpose of presenting key information in a quickly ingestible format.

After our discussions, the technicians said those points were "nice to haves" but agreed that they didn't bring any real value to the dashboard. We aligned on optimizing the dashboard to prioritize only impactful data.

Quality of Life Improvements in the New Design

In the wireframes, I communicated the use of common interaction mechanisms to better help users achieve their goals.

  • Introduced the abilities to filter, search, and sort in tables.
  • Correction in the type of affordances including when to use toggles, dropdowns, modals, and filters.
  • Bulk actions rather than to update settings individually
  • Confirmation messages after users have taken action

The redesigned Controlco web application

the delivery and impact

Introducing Controlco's First User-centric Platform

The web app was first tested with dummy data to ensure that the frontend communicated to the proper unit networks. After 3 months of working on the project, the platform was made live to the facility technicians.

The app looks better, but is it functionally better?

After its launch, the new Controlco app was met with success. Users called the new design "refreshing" and claimed that it was more performant. While we garnered a lot of positive feedback from users, we also wanted to evaluate the impact via metrics. We tracked how long it took for users to complete certain duties, such as changing overrides on equipment, setting up event schedules, and confirming how many units were occupied. With the new design and dashboard specifically, users were unsurprisingly spending less than half the time they were originally spending.

"I was just chatting with (customer name) the other day, and they really like the new design. I agree. This interface is way better. It's a lot cleaner and reminds me of an iPad app that my 4 year old daughter plays with." *laughs*
