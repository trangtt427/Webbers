Controlco

Product Design | Visual Design

The Controlco web app is a systems integration platform that lets facility technicians harness the full potential of their IoT data to ease through day to day operations. I took part in an ambitious redesign initiative that not only delivered an intuitive and empowering experience that users raved about, but also made a business impact in gaining more industry attention.

opportunity

High Time for a Redesign

Controlco integrates IoT systems to provide a platform for field technicians to manage HVAC, lighting, energy, and other data. However, the product was piecemealed together without design or a deep understanding of user behaviors and needs, resulting in a Frankenstein app that users griped about.

One of our customers was integrating lighting control panels, which meant a new feature was to be added in product to utilize that data. The VP of Product wanted to also use that opportunity for a redesign that would finally address all the current product flaws.

The state of the Controlco web app at the time

the role of design

As the design lead, I helped us shift towards a user-first approach and introduced industry-standard design tools for prototyping and collaboration.

I partnered with a fullstack engineer and a project manager, and led the project from end to end. I conducted user research, proposed solutions through mockups and iterating, owned the visual design, and measured our successes after implementation. I brought Sketch, Zeplin, and InVision into our tech stack.

research and discoveries

Interviewing the Experts

The users are facility technicians (also called controls/BAS technicians) and have a vast and blended knowledge of HVAC, lighting, mechanical, and systems programming. I conducted interviews with three technicians to capture their sentiments and frustrations about the app, understand their workflow, and find areas of improvement.

A Day in the Life of a Facility Technician

The technician's product touchpoint begins when they log into the Controlco web app to access their facility's data. They oversee equipment statuses, fan speeds, damper positions, temperatures, airflows, and other important information.

The "Home" and "HVAC" screen. The homepage for any IoT product is usually where users would see an overall summary of their facility, which was something that Controlco didn't supply.

The technicians play an important role in ensuring that their facilities are running smoothly in an optimal environment. Through the UI, they can change setpoints and positions for equipment as well as set up one-off events to override the current schedule. Controlco also provides reporting so they can keep track of the analytics. While the app helped get the job done, technicians had a bone to pick with the current interface and were quick to point out the areas of friction.

Pain Points

Information displayed was not prioritized to help users see what was important.
Whenever a user made an update on the frontend, there was no confirmation to indicate that the change took place.
There were too many small interactions needed for simple tasks such as setting up an equipment schedule.
Basic table functionalities were lacking, such as sorting and filtering in tables.

The most pressing area of concern was that users lacked a "health dashboard". It was important for users to see how their system is running at a high level so they can drill down into the areas that warrant attention. Additionally, users didn't enjoy the look and feel of the app, specifically pointing out that there were different fonts being used all over and that each page looked wildly different from one other.

Vision

Our goal was to redesign the Controlco web app to increase efficiencies through an improved experience.

Criteria for Success

  1. Provide a homepage dashboard that guide users towards what actions they should take in regards to the overall system
  2. Revamp the product to have a cohesive visual identity to make for a better experience
  3. Improvement in user workflow

design

Technicians wanted an overview of how their facility is doing.

Insights from the interviews showed that users were interested in things like:

  1. How many units are on/offline?
  2. What alarms were activated?
  3. What's the status of the central plant components?

My vision was to make our product easy to use, starting with reducing the amount of "processing" that people had to do when consuming heavy data. I created wireframes for a dashboard that captures the important KPIs to help users determine their next move.

Homepage Wireframe

The homepage would serve as the northstar for our users. Upon logging in, they'd be able to identify areas of interest and then dive into each system if needed.

There were several "quality of life" improvements that we wanted to address in the app.

Some improvements include functionalities that would help users navigate through dense tables, toggle inputs instead of dropdowns when appropriate, confirmation messages, and improved modal implementation.

Visual Design

We ventured away from our dark palette of blues and blacks. I played around with different color combinations and eventually captured a set of colors that were versatile and user-approved.

In spirit of making sure everything is digestible for users, we got rid of the serif fonts and went with Avenir for its nicely rounded shapes and readability.

A few newly redesigned components to bear our new aesthetic

the delivery

A New Look for Controlco

The app looks good, but is it better?

After its launch, the new Controlco app garnered a lot of positive feedback from users and internal stakeholders alike. In order to quantify the impact of our efforts, we went ahead and recorded the time it took for users to set up schedules to compare with the times they initially spent with the old interface. We managed to reduce the time users were spending on this task.

As a result of the redesign, one of our customers wanted to use Controlco as the systems integrator for a few other facilities in their portfolio. My biggest personal achievement, however, was helping the company pivot towards being design-oriented and user-centric, which was reflected in how we proceeded with subsequent projects.
