Controlco

Web Solution for Smart Buildings

Product design | user research | visual design

With the Controlco web platform, technicians can unlock the full potential of their IoT data to easily manage their facilities. However, users didn't feel empowered because of the product's lack of a polish and inconsistent user experience. I led an initiative of redesign the platform to bring users an enhanced experience with key quality-of-life improvements.

opportunity

"It helps me get the job done, but there are lot of things I'd like to see improved in this app."

Controlco integrates HVAC, lighting, energy, and other systems into a web platform that technicians can use to operate their facilities. Unfortunately, features were piecemealed together without strategy and fully understanding user needs, resulting in a classic Frankenstein product. Information was cluttered and not prioritized, basic functionalities were missing, the visual design was unappealing— all of this made for a product experience that users complained about.

The Controlco interface before the redesign.

"Some users were saying how tedious some of these tasks are, like changing equipment settings." - Controlco Data Modeler recalling a user complaint.

"It would be helpful if I had a system-level view of my facility. I don't know why we don't have something like that for Controlco. Other BAS software has it." - Field Technician

I was tasked with redesigning the Controlco app to uplift the experience and address the current user challenges. In having a better product, we would be able to improve customer loyalty with our existing clientele while gain more contracts with real estate firms.

the role of design

As the design lead, I owned the redesign process of our flagship web app from end to end.

Research

Conducted video calls with users to explore workflows, capture pain points, and solicit feedback regularly.

Information Architecture

Architected the platform to have better information hierarchy, delivering the most important data to users more clearly.

UX, UI, & Visual Design

Led interaction improvements across the platform and came up with our first modular component library.

Design Strategy

Introduced industry standard design tools and processes into the company and helped shift towards user-first methodologies.

I teamed up with a project manager and a lead engineer to deliver the redesign. Other stakeholders included the data modelers (internal team who interfaced with Controlco users) and the VP of product who was involved in design critiques.

research and discoveries

Diving Into the Controlco User's Workflow

I met with three different facility technicians who work at the real estate firm that we were building for. The facility technicians (also called controls/BAS technicians) and have a vast and blended knowledge of HVAC, lighting, mechanical, and systems programming. Their role is to keep facilities running in an optimal environment by regularly changing settings of their equipment.

I facilitated these sessions to understand their actions in the Controlco app, capture their sentiments and frustrations, and identify gaps for improvement.

The Field Technician

Age

40

Job Title

Facility Technician

Role

Responsible for preventative maintenance work on equipment, troubleshooting malfunctions via programming, and making sure everything is functioning as they should.

Challenges

  • No high level view of facility's health
  • Simple tasks feel tedious
  • Error prone interface
  • Current interface is not intuitive to new hires

Behaviors

Detail-oriented, optimizes for time, and does not consider himself as "tech-savvy". Likes to poke fun at millennials.

Insights showed that some parts of the users' flow were not streamlined to help them make decisions.

Users felt that the app lacked intuitive patterns that they've seen in modern day apps. These design faux pas amounted to a draining experience for users to have to carry out certain tasks.

  • There were bits of information that should be visually prioritized, such as being able to see equipment statuses and alarms. However, the only way to see them was to manually go to each system's tab in the navigation.
  • Too many extraneous interactions made simple tasks too tedious. Dropdowns were being used for inputs with only two options, users had to confirm a setting update one at a time, and there were no basic sorting or searching functionalities in tables.
  • The experience was disruptive and inconsistent. Each page looked different and several fonts were being used and were hard to read at times.

Uncovering What Users Really Wanted

Our discoveries led us to believe that users wanted a didactic platform. The most pressing area of friction for users was that they lacked the visibility of seeing an overall "health" on their system. Having a feature like this would give users guidance on where they should be focusing their attention and what to do next.

Research revealed that users were interested in knowing:

  1. How many units are on/offline.
  2. What alarms were activated.
  3. What the status is of each equipment.

In addition to wanting a sense of guidance for their in-app actions, users shared their feelings of dread when interacting with the app. Despite working in an industry where design is not prioritized, the facility technicians still wanted the app to be intuitive and engaging so that their workflow was more pleasant.

Vision

Our goal was to redesign the Controlco web app to increase productivity through delightful experiences catered to users.

Criteria for Success

  1. Provide a way that can better inform users the overall health of their system and what to do in their workflow
  2. Improve the interaction patterns so tasks are intuitive
  3. Revamp each page of the core product to have a cohesive visual identity

design

Conceptualizing a Homepage Dashboard

The Controlco platform was clearly remiss in showing users a high level view of their system. In spirit of providing users more guidance, I proposed a homepage dashboard that prioritized KPIs that would help users understand how their system was behaving. With this information, the next step for users will be for them to drill down into the equipment for assessment.

Homepage Wireframe

The homepage would serve as the northstar for our users. Upon logging in, they'd be able to identify areas of interest and then dive into each system if needed.

At first, technicians said that they needed to see a lot of certain data points on the dashboard.

I picked their brains about what type of data they wanted to see and if it would truly be beneficial for them. I wanted to make sure that the dashboard was not convoluted with KPIs that meant nothing in their workflow. In the end, the facility technicians agreed that the information that they were used to seeing was not entirely helpful after all. It was just something they were used to seeing. We aligned on optimizing the dashboard to show the most impactful data and make it easy to ingest at a glance.

Quality of Life Improvements in the New Design

Some improvements include functionalities that would help users navigate through dense tables, toggle inputs instead of dropdowns when appropriate, confirmation messages, and improved modal implementation. Check out the low fidelity prototype here.

Visual Design

We ventured away from our dark palette of blues and blacks, which were our go-to colors for our other clients, at the discretion of our VP of Product. We wanted to associate a brand new palette of lighter colors to the uplifting of our product. I played around with different color combinations and eventually captured a set of colors that were versatile and user-approved.

Users complained about the serif fonts being too hard to read in tables. We decided to go with Avenior for its nicely rounded shapes and readability.

the delivery

A New Look for Controlco

The app looks good, but is it better?

After its launch, the new Controlco app garnered a lot of positive feedback from users and internal stakeholders alike. In order to quantify the impact of our efforts, we went ahead and recorded the time it took for users to set up schedules to compare with the times they initially spent with the old interface. We managed to reduce the time users were spending on this task.

As a result of the redesign, one of our customers wanted to use Controlco as the systems integrator for a few other facilities in their portfolio. My biggest personal achievement, however, was helping the company pivot towards being design-oriented and user-centric, which was reflected in how we proceeded with subsequent projects.
