Controlco

Web Solution for Smart Buildings

Product design | user research | visual design

With the Controlco web platform, technicians can unlock the full potential of their IoT data to easily manage their facilities. However, users came across various areas of friction because of the product's lack of a polish and inconsistent user experience. I led the platform redesign to bring users an enhanced experience with key quality-of-life improvements and increase contract deals for Controlco.

opportunity

"It helps me get the job done, but there are a lot of things I'd like to see improved in this app."

Controlco integrates HVAC, lighting, energy, and other systems into a centralized platform where all of that data is made accessible and users can interact with it. Unfortunately, features were piecemealed together without strategy and fully understanding user needs, resulting in a classic Frankenstein product. Information was cluttered and not prioritized, basic functionalities were missing, the visual design was unappealing— all of this made for a product experience that users complained about.

The Controlco interface before the redesign.

"Some users were saying how much of a chore it was to do something so simple, like changing equipment settings." - Controlco Data Modeler recalling a user complaint.

"It would be helpful if I can see all the ongoing alarms and equipment statuses in one place. I don't know why we don't have something like that for Controlco. Other BAS software has it." - Field Technician

I was tasked with redesigning the Controlco app to uplift the experience and address the current user challenges. Our VP of Product prioritized this because we wanted to improve customer loyalty with our existing clientele while gain more contracts with real estate firms.

the role of design

As the design lead, I owned the redesign of our flagship web app from end to end.

Research

Conducted video calls with facility technicians to understand their day-to-day, capture pain points, and solicit feedback regularly.

Information Architecture

Architected the platform to have better information hierarchy, delivering the most important data to users more clearly.

UX, UI, & Visual Design

Led interaction improvements across the platform and came up with our first modular component library.

Design Strategy

Introduced industry standard design tools and processes into the company and helped shift towards user-first methodologies.

I teamed up with a project manager and a lead engineer to deliver the redesign. Other stakeholders included the data modelers (internal team who interfaced with Controlco users) and the VP of product who was intermittently involved in the syncs.

research and discoveries

Diving Into the Controlco User's Workflow

I met with three different facility technicians who work at the real estate firm that we were building for. The facility technicians (also called controls/BAS technicians) have a vast and blended knowledge of HVAC, lighting, mechanical, and systems programming. Their role is to keep facilities running in an optimal environment.

I facilitated these sessions to understand their actions in the Controlco app, capture their sentiments and frustrations, and identify gaps for improvement.

The Field Technician

Age

40

Job Title

Facility Technician

Role

Responsible for preventative maintenance work on equipment, troubleshooting malfunctions via programming, and making sure everything is functioning as they should.

Challenges

  • No high level view of facility's health
  • Tasks feel tedious
  • Error prone interface
  • Current interface is not intuitive to new hires

Behaviors

Detail-oriented, optimizes for time, and does not consider himself as "tech-savvy". Likes to poke fun at millennials.

Insights showed that some parts of the users' flow were not streamlined to help them make decisions efficiently.

Users felt that the app lacked the common patterns that they've seen in other apps. These design faux pas amounted to a poor experience for users as they carry out simple tasks.

  • Key data points were not being visually prioritized, such as being able to see equipment statuses and alarms. The the only way to see them was to manually go to each system's tab in the navigation.
  • Extraneous steps were needed in order to update a setting. Dropdowns were being used for inputs with only two options, users can't make bulk changes, and there were no basic sorting or searching functionalities in tables.
  • The experience was disruptive and inconsistent. Each page looked like they came from different apps and several fonts were being used.

Uncovering What Users Really Wanted

Our discoveries led us to believe that users wanted a didactic platform. The biggest challenge for users was that they lacked the visibility of seeing the "health" of their facility. Having a feature like this would instruct users on where they should be focusing their attention and what to do next.

Research revealed that users were interested in knowing:

  1. How many units are on/offline.
  2. What alarms were activated.
  3. Are there any statuses users should be aware of.

In addition to wanting a sense of guidance for their in-app actions, users shared their feelings of dread when interacting with the app. Despite working in an industry where design is not prioritized, the facility technicians still wanted the app to be intuitive and engaging so that their assignments could be more pleasant.

Vision

Our goal was to redesign the Controlco web app to increase productivity through delightful experiences catered to users.

Criteria for Success

  1. Provide a way that can better inform users the overall health of their system and what they need to achieve
  2. Improve the interaction patterns they can handle their tasks quickly and efficiently
  3. Revamp each page of the core product to have a cohesive visual identity

design

Conceptualizing a Homepage Dashboard

The Controlco platform was remiss in giving users a big picture view of their facility. In spirit of providing users more direction, I proposed a homepage dashboard that prioritized KPIs that would help users understand how their system was behaving. With this information, the next step for users would be to drill down into faulty equipment for assessment.

Homepage Wireframe

Upon logging in, users would be able to identify areas of interest and then dive into each system if needed instead of going to each system manually to figure out what's wrong.

At first, technicians said that they needed specific data points on the dashboard.

I picked their brains about what KPIs users wanted to see and in what ways would it beneficial. I wanted to make sure that the dashboard was not convoluted with KPIs that would not affect their workflow. The tradeoff of putting many KPIs meant that the dashboard would lose its purpose of prioritizing the valuable data points in palatable visualization.

After further discussions, the technicians said those points were "nice to haves" but agreed that they didn't bring any real value to the dashboard. We aligned on optimizing the dashboard to show the most impactful data and make it easy to ingest at a glance.

Quality of Life Improvements in the New Design

Basic UI principles were introduced to alleviate the steps needed for users to achieve their goals.

  • Introduced the abilities to filter, search, and sort into tables to help users find data that they needed.
  • Correction in the type of affordances including when to use toggles, dropdowns, modals, and filters.
  • A single action to confirm multiple changes rather than having to confirm each time a setting was changed.
  • Messaging that appears after certain changes have taken place on the backend.

Visual Design

We ventured away from our usual dark palette of blues and blacks at the discretion of our VP of Product. We wanted to associate a brand new palette of lighter colors to the uplifting of our product. I played around with different color combinations and eventually captured a set of colors that were versatile and user-approved.

Users complained about the serif fonts being too hard to read in tables. We decided to go with Avenir because it sat well in tables with a lot of information.

the delivery and impact

Introducing Controlco's First User-centric Platform

The product was tested with dummy data to ensure that the frontend interactions communicated to the proper units. After 3 months of working on the project, the platform was made live to the facility technicians for one of our customer's properties.

The app looks better, but is it functionally better?

After its launch, the new Controlco app was met with success. We garnered a lot of positive feedback from users, but we also wanted to evaluate the impact of our efforts beyond qualitative metrics. I observed how long it took for users to complete certain duties, such as changing overrides on "dummy equipment", setting up event schedules, and confirming how many units were occupied. With the new design, users were spending less than half the time they were originally spending in the old UI. Users especially liked the new dashboard and the improvements in the HVAC page.

"This interface is way better. It's a lot cleaner and reminds me of an iPad app that my 4 year old daughter plays with." - Controlco Data Modeler

As a result of our accomplishments, this specific customer wanted to use the newly designed platform for a few other facilities in their portfolio. We ended up closing a contract to enable that. Even though our outward impact was large, my biggest personal achievement was helping the company pivot towards being user-focused. For all of our subsequent projects, we used the same framework and processes to approach our problems.
