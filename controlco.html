Controlco offers commercial HVAC management solutions. I took the company to new design frontiers by revamping the products and adding user-centric processes.

Role

  • Interaction design, UI design
  • Designed 3D floorplans using AutoCAD and SketchUp

Results

Closed a deal with a prominent real estate entity

Engineers gained back 10 hours of productivity per month due to improved internal design-led processes

Application

Web + mobile web

Opportunity

Controlco was contracted to integrate HVAC systems for a prominent customer. Leadership wanted to use that chance to modernize the product experience.

Most Controlco customers are used to the old-fashioned products and processes of the slowly changing HVAC industry, but the company was ready to make UX a competitive advantage. Modern clients, internal support teams, and prospects have already taken jabs at the product feeling and looking like a program from the 90s.

Product development at Controlco was a top-down process. Requirements funneled from the CEO to engineers to interpret.

Projects lacked vision and strategy; engineers didn't have the insights to know what users needed and were constantly scrapping their code upon any type of feedback. There was no discovery or design. All of this led to a fragmented product experience for users, and for engineers, a frustrating way of working.

Discovery

Client calls used to be driven solely by the project manager, but design involvement became standard as I promoted discussions for candid customer feedback.

Our main users, field technicians, joined these calls and offered feedback and detailed accounts of the work they do. Their job is to maintain a comfortable environment for malls, data centers, and high-rises. While they've admitted that tech isn't their forte, they know their way around programming IoT equipment.

A few of the tasks that technicians are responsible for

The field technician's day-to-day rarely changes. Using the software, they're looking through tables of data to see if any of their air handler units, lighting panels, and alarms are performing outside of optimal conditions. If anything is off, they'd have to troubleshoot the device and program it manually.

In the less ideal situations, users had to swim through poorly architected data visualizations to find anomalies and get used to interaction affordances that didn't match their expectations. There were also no safeguards for dangerous system-wide actions.

Design

Quality of Life Improvements

I purged the product of clunky behaviors that didn't make sense (dropdowns for boolean inputs, confusing filter patterns, etc.) and introduced a better navigation experience through recognizable patterns. To reduce the chance of those system-wide human errors, I introduced confirmation dialogs and visually de-emphasized risky actions in the UI.

Since getting quality data quickly was a point of friction, I explored ideas for a homepage dashboard with KPIs. Previously, users were plowing through all of their HVAC and lighting panel data and relying on subtle UI elements to flag exceptions. I worked with users to organize the information and give priority to data that could inform of potential system-wide failures, like malfunctioning water tanks and chiller towers.

We showed technicians wireframe concepts to help them get acquainted to new patterns while getting feedback.

Users were kept in the loop and got to experience working with prototypes to offer feedback for refinement, a process that was unfamiliar to them but sorely welcomed and appreciated. We saw success with design efforts taking place upstream to understand our users better. We were obtaining feedback on wireframes and validating ideas more frequently with users before engineers jumped into the code.

A Fresh New Look and Modern Feel

I established new design patterns and componentized them to be used for other products. We were able to deviate from the archaic color scheme and bring on vibrant, approachable colors with meaning.

Outcomes

The delivered product was a success; our contract got extended and the client wanted to continue using Controlco's services to integrate other buildings in their portfolio.

"It kinda reminds me of this iPad game that my 5-year old plays. Not a bad thing though! I'm saying it's very simple and pretty."

- Equipment Programmer

Another win from this project was that we found our tempo working with design upstream to get requirements crisp for engineers and solutions tailored for users.

Engineers used to spend about 3 hours a week consulting with the CEO and developing features that were subpar for users. Now, they get time back to work on requirements that align closely to what users actually want, and are a part of a more inclusive team structure. With this strategy, we're able to work towards a more defined vision and consistently deliver a cohesive and elegant experience for users.
