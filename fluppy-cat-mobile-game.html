Fluppy Cat Mobile Game

Product Design

A simple iOS game designed to test your endurance and patience

About Fluppy Cat

After spending a whole week experimenting with different mobile games, my boyfriend and I were inspired to create our own. It was an opportunity to work on something that combined our passions in code, design, and games.

Fluppy Cat: A Game of Patience

We wanted to make a game that had a lot of replay value and was easy to get into. We were inspired by Flappy Bird, a side scroller where the player has to tap on the screen repeatedly to keep the sprite hovering as they advance through the stages. Our game was going to be a playful knock-off of that but based off a tribute to my late cat Pibby. After a brainstorming session, we decided that the premise would be about Pibby flying through dark caverns on a blankey to collect magical fishbone orbs.

The iOS App Requirements

Using Trello, we set up a punchlist of things we needed from each other. Since we both owned iPhones, we could only build for Apple so that we can fully test it at home. I did some research about iOS guidelines for their apps and learned for every asset, I would need to create 3 versions: 1x, 2x, and 3x.

The following assets were needed:

  1. Sprite component: This was going to be Pibby on his blankey.
  2. Backdrop: Since Pibby is orange, it would be a good contrast to put him in a dark environment to make him stand out. We decided on a purple background.
  3. Obstacles: The challenge of this game is being to navigate between obstacles as Pibby flies across the screen. We settled on stalactites and stalagmites.
  4. Score: In order to increase the player's score, Pibby would have to collect glowing fishbone orbs for points.
  5. iOS components for app: We also needed an app icon optimized for the iOS homescreen, and a logo for the title screen.

Creating the Artwork

I made some whiteboard doodles of the various objects in Fluppy Cat, keeping in mind that everything needed to be simple and digestible on a small screen. The final assets were drawn in Illustrator and Photoshop.

Protagonist sprite (Pibby), magic fish orbs, and stalactite/stalagmites.

The image which would be made to scroll to generate the backdrop

Built and Ready to Play

The game was built with xcode and we even implemented sound effects! When it was time to test the game however, there was this incredible lag during the gameplay. It turns out that the lag was caused by the spikes. We read up on it and learned that in the world of game design, triangles are actually modified rectangles and are difficult to generate. Another thing we realized that it was also difficult to see the sharp points of the the tapered columns, making the game extremely hard. It was back to the drawing board to come up with a new idea.

The spikes were hard to see and they generated a delay when playing

Removing the Spikes to Improve Performance

To optimize the game performance and make the obstacles visible, I changed the spikes into crystals. It made just as much sense as the stalactites because crystals also form in caves. With their color and shape, it brought a nice contrast against the purple background. Thanks to their rectangular shape, there was no more lag.

The improvement: more contrast and no lag

Testing Again

We further tested the game with our coworkers. While there were no longer any delays in generating the crystal pillars, people found the game to be difficult still. They claimed that Pibby was too big and had difficulty moving him between the narrow openings between crystals. We solved this by slowing down the rate at which the pillars were generated and making Pibby smaller.

Fluppy Cat!

The Takeaway: Thoughts and Lessons

This was the first iOS-specific app that I designed. It was a fun project that allowed me to learn more about the requirements when designing for the iPhone. It also encouraged me to work on more mobile apps. Something I learned was that as a designer, I get tunnel-vision with the things I create. It's not until you allow a fresh pair of eyes to review your products to see if there are some issues. In this case, having other people play Fluppy Cat helped me realize that the game was initially a bit too difficult.
