Fluppy Cat

mobile game for ios

An iOS side-scrolling game dedicated to my late cat Pibby. Made to test your dexterity and patience.

At one point in my life, I was downloading a bunch of mobile games to try out. I played quite a few, but one of them was very memorable: Flappybird. I teamed up with an engineer to build our own mobile game based on the Flappybird formula. Players would repeatedly tap on the screen to keep their character from colliding. The game was based on my late orange tabby cat Pibby. Players would control Pibby to help him navigate through a cavern while collecting magical fishbone orbs.

I drew all of the assets including the sprite, orbs, obstacles, and background. The game went through some iterations after testing it out with some players, in which we had to make some tweaks to reduce lag and difficulty. The game was made available only for iOS (I was an iPhone loyalist at the time) and built in Xcode.

Pibby, magical fishbone orbs, and cavern spikes.

The backdrop would repeatedly scroll to simulate the movement of Pibby traveling.

The purple spikes posed a few problems: 1. it was hard to see and 2. triangles are hard to code, therefore causing lag in the game. The solution was to replace them with rectangular obstacles.

Invitely

mobile app design

A quarantine pet project to help people plan events during Covid.

Invitely lets you create and manage invitations for events straight from your phone. I started the project because I had a bone to pick with other invitation apps after having been invited to a virtual baby shower. I've fleshed out the designs for the concept and development is in early phases.
