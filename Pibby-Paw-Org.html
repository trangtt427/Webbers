Pibby Paw Org
A pet adoption website that connects people with sheltered cats, resources, and news in the cat community.

Role:
UI Designer | UX Designer
Tools:
Sketch | InVision | Overflow.io
Processes:
Wireframing | Hi Fi Mockup | Prototyping
Date:
May 2018

Project Overview

As one of the requirements for UCSD's UX Design Certificate Program, I had to design a website for the use of either ecommerce, travel booking, or pet adoptions. Being the passionate cat-lover that I am, I went with the latter.

The required pages for the assignment include:

  • Homepage
  • Content submission
  • Resources
  • Help section
  • Search feature
  • One internal page of my choice

The assignment took a month to complete as I was communicating with my instructor to get feedback at every step of the process.

Wireframing and Common Web Interface Patterns

The kick-off of this project began with a pitch to my professor. After getting the approval, I designed a wireframe to showcase all the required features and where they appear. The artboards were exported to an Overflow.io protoype where one can interact and go through the flow, screen by screen.

The design would feature several common web interface patterns:

  • Search feature to find pets on the homepage
  • Autocomplete paired with the search function
  • Dropdowns paired with filters
  • Virtual scrolling for testimonies (carousel with dots)
  • Pagination for search results
  • Interstitial/modal/dialogue box

Implementing these common design patterns help the website serve its purpose to visitors. The main use case that I designed for includes prospect adopters, where the search feature is far more important than any other directive for them.

Visual Design

At this step of the process, I was required to submit a design comp of the homepage for review. During this project, I was inspired by my late orange-tabby cat named Pibby. The color palette I came up with uses an orange gradient and a blue to contrast. It was important to make the website feel friendly and bold to visitors, but also to include enough white space when necessary.

A snippet of the colors and typography

Final Submission

As the last part of the assignment, I submitted an InVision prototype and a word document to explain the rationale behind my design choices. Also, I implemented some of the instructor's feedback in the final submission.

The final submission for the course

Beyond the Course

Through this project, I was given the opportunity to explore every step of the design process, drawing a lot from my academic knowledge. I received a lot of critique from my professor, which really had me thinking about other avenues I could've taken with the design. Overall, it was an assignment that satisfied my creative yearnings. I've become more aware of what web interface patterns to use thanks to this project.

Although it was a requirement for my coursework, I would love to bring this website to life in the future. It would allow more opportunities for collaboration, meaning there would be refinements and changes.
