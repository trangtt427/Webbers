The Design Journey

My career trajectory was a bit different from most designers in the industry. Despite going hard in the paint towards becoming a healthcare professional, my passion for art came full circle. Here's how it all came together for me.

Artistic Beginnings

At 3 years old, I was already showing an affinity for the arts. I was drawing, painting, and crafting trinkets. I even made it on television after winning a county-wide art contest when I was 11 years old.

Biology

Science was my favorite subject in school. I became interested in genetics, medicine, human disease and psychology. I enjoyed learning about what makes up our bodies chemically, what makes us behave the way we do, and how we interact with others. I graduated with a B.S. in Biology from UCSB.

Visual Design

Even while pursuing Biology, I kept my creative chops fresh with art courses and through designing digital collateral whenever I got the chance. Throughout college and after, I took on various freelance projects. It was clear that my strength and passion lie in visual design, so I made the shift.

Web Design

I got into designing for web technologies and learned more about the world of development. During this time, I taught myself how to write HTML/CSS and use industry standard design tools.

UX/UI Design

I began taking a holistic approach to design, focusing on every step of the process. I kept users at the forefront of my brain and made sure that my design was empathetic to their needs. To fortify my foundation, I enrolled in a one year program at UCSD to receive my User Experience Design Certificate.

UX design is the culmination of my lifelong pursuit of science and creativity. When people think about design, the first thing that comes to mind are the pretty visuals. UX design is more of a science; it's built on research, data, and testing, which I've become familiar with throughout studying Biology. It's exciting to be able to pick apart problems and provide creative solutions to help people in the digital space.

Currently, I'm putting my biology background and design skills to work at Bio-Rad.
