Bio-Rad: Marcom Designs

Product Design & Development

Bio-Rad 2018 - 2019
From building code and design libraries to internal tools, our small but mighty team helped propel Bio-Rad's Marcom team towards scalable solutions.

About Bio-Rad

Bio‑Rad is a manufacturer of products for life science research and clinical diagnostics markets. In the past few years, Bio‑Rad began focusing on creating a modern customer experience beyond just desktop. The Global Marcom team channeled their efforts into building a responsive microsite to showcase their top-selling systems from the Clinical Diagnostics Group (CDG) and Life Sciences Group (LSG).

The Problem

Bio‑Rad is not known to have the latest standards in design and development because it's not their focus. As the company attitudes shifted, they began emphasizing more on user experience. I was part of a small but mighty web team tasked with creating a solutions microsite to showcase all of Bio‑Rad's top-selling products. To do that, we had to first establish design and code guidelines so that we can educate and distribute amongst the other teams within Marcom.

Role

I was the main UX designer of the Marcom web team and worked with one other developer. Our responsibilities include designing and building campaign pages and syncing them up with Marketo, and maintaining our design and code documentation. Besides the microsite, I also supported the marketing team by designing and building internal tools. I worked closely with PMs, art directors, and a copywriter.

Who's the Audience?

We have two types of users of our projects: customers and the internal marketing/sales team. Our customers are scientists and clinical diagnostics specialists who are not that tech savvy still use Internet Explorer (yikes). They are the main consumers of the microsite who make key decisions on what products to purchase. We also needed to support the marketing/sales with internal tools to help them locate maintain their campaign resources.

As CMO, Amar has a lot of decision-making power when it comes to purchasing equipment for his lab. He does not have a good grasp on web technologies, therefore uses Internet Explorer as his default browser. He doesn't have a clue where to go to learn about Bio‑Rad equipment.

Goals

Wants to quickly get information about the best products all in one place. Does not want to rely on brochures and print collateral.

Behaviors and Beliefs

Gets overwhelmed with new technology easily. Feels that upgrading technology to the most current is a lot of work.

The Marcom Design Library

We inherited a design system that was still a work in progress. Because about a third of our users browse our material with Internet Explorer, we couldn't use modern browser-supported capabilities like flexbox and grid. Instead, we created a proprietary component library. My team maintained this system and shared the template with the other teams within the department.

Text and button styles

While the microsite would assume its own brand style, we stayed within the boundaries to make sure that it was easily recognizable with the Bio‑Rad brand. We added more components as well as simpler code into the guide. My team was also involved in educating the other marketing departments to show them how to use our templates. The leading Global Marcom Team adopted our template and are customizing it for their landing pages.

Sitemap for Bio-Rad Solutions Website

We worked closely with product managers to define the navigation

We worked in constant collaboration with multiple project managers who were each in charge of a different product line. Although the website can be visited globally, not all countries/regions will have access to every equipment on the website. To support our users, we created two branches for the website; one for the US and one for outside the US (OUS). Although scrappy, a functional gating system was was built from scratch to help redirect users to the right products. This was the optimal experience for users because it would prevent them from seeing products that they cannot purchase.

Product Messaging

The internal copywriter was responsible for all the content and messaging on our pages. Once that version is done, we use ProofHQ to share it with the marketing team to get their copy edits. The approved iteration then goes to Regulatory.

We went through several approval steps to finalize content

Hosting on Marketo

Our code was built locally and then hosted on Marketo where logic is linked up to track engagement from users. There are technical limitations working within Marketo due to their poor developer environment. To combat this, our microsite was developed with a few hacks to overwrite some of Marketo's baked-in functions.

Introducing Bio-Rad's First Responsive Website

The microsite was launched in early February and received global praise from within the different departments of the company. This was a huge milestone as it showed customers that the company is now pushing towards a better user experience.

The website showcased the products, solutions, and resources for both the Clinical Diagnostics Group and Life Sciences Group. It was the all-in-one location to unify both departments to help customers make informed decisions on purchasing.

The Takeaway: Thoughts and Lessons

One of the biggest struggles we had was trying to help the team break away from the slow-moving and antiquated processes of Bio‑Rad. At times, it took a lot of effort to defend and explain our design and dev decisions to stakeholders and fellow marcom team members. Luckily, I was able to work within a nimble team and gain support from my manager to pursue what we thought was best for our users.
