Creating a Game With a Low Barrier to Entry

We wanted to make a game that had a lot of replay value and was easy to get into. The game was going to be a variation of Flappy Bird, a side scroller where the player has to tap on the screen repeatedly to keep the sprite hovering. The main character of the game would be an orange cat as a tribute to my late tabby, Pibby. It was a nonsensical idea but we were going to make Pibby fly on a magic blanket, navigating between stalactites and stalagmites to collect fishbone orbs.

Using Trello, we set up a punchlist of things we needed from each other. The look and feel of the game relied heavily on the art style and colors in which I managed. We were building it out for the iPhone so I had to follow specific iOS guidelines for different screen sizes. For every asset, I would need to create 3 versions: 1x, 2x, and 3x.

The following assets were needed:

  1. Character design: I brainstormed how I wanted Pibby to look and what art style to use.
  2. Backdrop: Because Pibby is orange, it would be a good contrast to put him in a dark environment to make him stand out. We decided on a dark purple cave theme.
  3. Obstacles: It made sense to have stalactites and stalagmites in the cave. As the player controls Pibby, they have to make sure he flies between the obstacles being generated in a horizontal scroll.
  4. Obstacles: It made sense to have stalactites and stalagmites in the cave. As the player controls Pibby, they have to make sure he flies between the obstacles being generated in a horizontal scroll.
  5. Score: In order to increase the player's score, Pibby would have to collect glowing fishorbs for points.
  6. App icon: We also needed an app icon optimzed for the iOS homescreen.
  7. Logo and title screen: The last thing we needed was a logo as well as a title screen that loads up when users open the app.
