Tactic

Tax Accounting for Digital Assets

Automated solutions to drastically reduce the time businesses spend on tax reporting for digital assets.

Summary

Led design for the entire product experience

Gave shape to the company's design function and spun up better documentation processes

Achieved 11 million in funding in September 2022

role

Product Design Consultant

timeframe

December 2021 - December 2022

application

Web

opportunity

Helping Accountants and Finance Teams Close Their Books

Crypto was starting to appear on more company balance sheets these days, so that meant more regulations and guidelines around how to report income on them. Not too many intuitive solutions were in the market yet, especially for companies that handle about 1,000+ transactions a month. Tactic, a start-up with just the 4 of us at the time, aimed to solve this. I joined as a design consultant, and with my partner-in-crime—the lead product manager, we delivered elaborate end-to-end solutions for b2b companies to manage crypto taxes in a clean, compliant way.

research

Learning About Taxes and Crypto

In the beginning, we poked around at what other companies were doing (mainly Cryptio, Koinly, and CryptoTaxCalculator) and identified the problems they were solving—and for what types of customers. We were constrained with resources, but had our own accountant and a few customer friendlies to learn from. I held Q+A sessions to better understand what accountants and finance stakeholders do ahead of tax season.

interesting insight

The crypto accounting process was very labor-intensive, and took one of our customers up to 40 hours a month.

One user, a Head of Finance, had his engineers pull transactions across different wallets and exchanges to manually calculate their cost-basis and fees. This tedious workflow took away valuable development time from engineers. Users made it clear that a treasury view of their data would be helpful.

Users reached out to the support team for certain workflows because there was no way to do it in the product yet

While users were able to connect their exchanges and wallets through a barebones interface, they didn't have product solutions to do more of the nuanced accounting tasks, like mapping their Quickbooks chart of accounts. Much of this was done by contacting Tactic and having the Product and Engineering teams handle this in the backend. It was manageable with the number of customers we had at the time, but would obviously not be sustainable as we grow.

desired outcomes

We wanted to empower users to self-onboard, configure their Tactic environment, and start getting on track for tax reporting.

solutions

All Crypto Financial Activity in One Place

We indexed hard on input from the few experts and customers we met with, and mocked up prototypes to put in front of real folks. I designed several features, with key solutions that gave users a high level view of their crypto activity and ways to organize that data.

The Transactions Page

We created the Transactions page to bring together financial activity from the user's various wallets and exchanges. Users would be able to view info like counterparties, fees, and nested token activity, and edit and attach documents and memos to provide context for anyone viewing this information.

Early prototype of the Transactions page—the bread and butter of the Tactic platform.

burning question

Now that users can see this information, what do they need to do with it?

One thing we learned was that users were often tidying up their transactions. Each transaction must be mapped to a category, usually based on directionality, so that it gets properly accounted for in their journals. Since this is a high frequency touchpoint, I designed a lightweight interaction where users can apply categories efficiently by doing it inline or via bulk.

In addition to directional categories, there are also sub-categories. For example, a transaction's direction could be considered "money in", but "buy", "income", and "airdrop" are sub-categories that fall under that. Since the system can only detect the direction, we made it easier for users to categorize by showing only the sub-categories based on that direction. If they really wanted to, users could also overwrite these in an advanced workflow.

Categorizing a transaction has never been easier!

Save hours by automating with rules

For customers with lots of transactions, categorizing each one would be a nightmare. We enhanced this feature even further, and came up with a way to automate this step based on conditions. I designed a Rules feature where the system performs an action (i.e. categorizing transactions or attaching a memo) if a transaction fulfills a certain criteria. Users have the choice to apply the rules retroactively as well, giving them full control of how they want to manage their transactions.

Create rules to have the system do the work for you.

Guided Flows to Connect Wallets and Exchanges

Connecting to a source is the first step to towards populating transaction data. We designed an easy wallet setup process; bite-sized and has helpful instructions.

Bite-sized steps to help users connect their data with low stress.

Set up Charts of Account within the product

Previously, users needed Tactic intervention to configure their ledger mapping. I designed an experience for them to map tokens to their appropriate ledger accounts, including ways to granularly map their assets to sub-accounts.

Users can fine-tune how they want their assets mapped to their ledgers.

On-demand Reporting

Working with accountants, we identified which types of reports are useful for tax preparation. We came up with a UI for users to generate those reports instantly to be leveraged for handoff between different stakeholders.

Early wireframe of the Reports page.

outcomes

Tactic Has Entered the Chat

I worked with three engineers who divided and conquered the tasks to diligently implement the designs. After the features were rolled out, customers started seeing a reduction in the time they were spending with tax management.

"It has helped tremendously to go from looking at 10,000 transactions to just one dashboard. Our finance resources are now able to spend time on mission-critical items like the payment infrastructure of our business."
"We may dedicate 8 to 10 hours a month to crypto accounting nowadays."
"There is very little, if any, clean up required and the UI and dashboard are something our accountants love too."

The features demoed incredibly well in sales calls, winning over a few customers who were impressed with the level of automation. After months of working heads down and thinking through more complex accounting challenges, we hit a string of milestones. We emerged from stealth in May 2022 with a seed funding of 2.6 million. In September, we raised 11 million in funding. The team has grown rapidly and is now serving dozens of customers across many different industries.
