opportunity

Designing in Uncharted Territories

In December 2021, I joined Tactic as the founding designer (employee #4) to drive a solution that helps controllers and tax accountants manage their crypto accounting. A growing number of companies started seeing crypto on their balance sheets. In response, there were more regulations and guidelines around how to report income on crypto activity. There weren't too many clean solutions in the market though, especially solutions needed for companies who had huge volumes of transactions.

Most of the complex design challenges we faced were around these types of customers —how might we allow users to resolve conflicts in their transactions? When a user categorizes their transactions with automation, should that apply to old transactions? What does the Quickbook support look like? I worked closely with accountants and Product to wrap my head around things I was hearing for the first time, like chart of accounts, journaling, and reconciliation, as well as crypto concepts like wallets and staking.

results

User-approved Solutions

While designing, I thought carefully about the decision-making behavior of users, and what might help lower the barrier of entry for setting up wallets and exchanges. While the product is tailored for businesses, we took a consumer-friendly approach towards ensuring a product experience that is simple to use, but includes adequate educational content and guidance for anyone to catch on. Early prototypes were shared with the few customer friendlies and rapidly refined until their major needs were met.
