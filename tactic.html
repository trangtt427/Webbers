Tactic

Tax Accounting for Digital Assets

Automated solutions to drastically reduce the time businesses spend on tax reporting for digital assets.

Project in a Nutshell

Led design for the entire product experience

Gave shape to the company's design function

Achieved 11 million in funding in September 2022

opportunity

Helping Accountants and Finance Stakeholders Close the Books

Crypto was starting to appear on more company balance sheets these days, so that meant more regulations and guidelines around how to report income on them. Not too many intuitive solutions were in the market yet, especially for companies that handle about 1,000+ transactions a month. Tactic aimed to offer solutions for these companies. I partnered very closely with the lead product manager to design end-to-end solutions for managing digital asset taxes in a clean and compliant way.

research

Learning About Taxes and Crypto

In the beginning, we poked around at what other companies were doing (mainly Cryptio, Koinly, and CryptoTaxCalculator) and identified the problems they were solving—and for what types of customers. We were constrained with resources, had our own accountant as well as a few customer friendlies to learn from. I held a few Q+A sessions to better understand what accountants and finance stakeholders do ahead of tax season.

interesting insight

The crypto accounting process was very labor-intensive, and took one of our customers up to 40 hours a month.

One user, a Head of Finance, had his engineers pulling transactions across different wallets and exchanges to manually calculate their cost-basis and fees. This tedious workflow took away valuable development time from engineers.

Users needed to contact our support team for certain workflows because there were no in-product solutions yet.

While users were able to connect their exchanges and wallets through a barebones interface, they didn't have product solutions to do more of the necessary accounting tasks, like mapping their Quickbooks chart of accounts. Much of this was done by contacting Tactic and having the Product and Engineering teams handle this in the backend. It was manageable with the number of customers we had at the time, but would obviously not be sustainable as we grow.

desired outcomes

We wanted to empower users to self-onboard, configure their Tactic environment, and start getting on track for tax reporting.

solutions

All Crypto Financial Activity in One Place

We indexed hard on input from the few experts and customers we met with, and mocked up prototypes to put in front of real folks. I designed several features, with key solutions that allowed users a full view of their money movement and ways to organize that data.

The Transactions Page

Users shared that properly labeling their transactions and contacts was a huge part of their day to day. We created the Transactions page to reveal financial activity from the user's various wallets and exchanges. Users would be able to edit some of these, which would then reflect in their company's accounting software (usually Quickbooks or Xero).

Early prototype of the Transactions page—the bread and butter of the Tactic platform.

burning question

Now that users can see this information, what do they need to do with it?

One thing we learned was that users often categorize their transactions. Each transaction must be mapped to a category, usually based on directionality, so that it gets properly accounted for in their journals. Since this is a high frequency touchpoint, I designed an interaction where users can categorize transactions individually inline or via bulk.

In addition to directional categories, there are also sub-categories. For example, a transaction's direction could be considered "money in", but buy, income, and airdrop are sub-categories that fall under that. Since the system can only detect the direction, we made it easier for users to categorize by showing only the sub-categories based on a direction. If they really wanted to, users could also change the direction in an advanced workflow.

Categorizing a transaction has never been easier!

Save hours by automating with rules

For customers with huge volumes of transactions, categorizing new transactions each time could get tiring. We took the feature a step further, and came up with a way to automate the categorizing of transactions based on conditions. I designed a Rules feature where the system will perform an action (i.e. categorizing transactions or attaching a memo) if a transaction fulfills a certain criteria. Users have the choice to apply the rules retroactively as well, giving them full control of how they want to manage their transactions.

Create rules to have the system do the work for you.

Guided Flows to Connect Wallets and Exchanges

Transaction data can come from wallets or exchange platforms. Users will now have a simple in-product workflow to easily connect their sources.

Bite-sized steps to help users connect their data with low stress.

Set up Charts of Account within the product

Previously, users needed human intervention to configure their ledger mapping. I designed an experience for them to map tokens to their appropriate ledger accounts, including ways to granuarly map their assets to sub-accounts.

Users can fine-tune how they want their assets mapped to their ledgers.

On-demand Reporting

We worked with accountants to identify which types of reports are useful for tax preparation. We created a UI for users to generate those reports instantly so that they can be leveraged for their many use cases.

Early wireframe of the Reports page.

outcomes

Tactic Has Entered the Chat

I worked with three engineers who divided and conquered the tasks to diligently implement the designs. After the features were rolled out, customers started seeing a reduction in their workfloads.

"There is very little, if any, clean up required and the UI and dashboard are something our accountants love too."

The features demoed incredibly well in sales calls, winning over a few customers who were impressed with the level of automation. After months of working heads down and thinking through more complex accounting challenges, we hit a string of milestones. We emerged from stealth in May 2022 with a seed funding of 2.6 million. In September, we raised 11 million in funding. The team has grown rapidly and is now serving dozens of customers, with a few having shared some major success stories recently.
