Controlco: IoT Web App

Enterprise platform allowing for buildings IoT analytics and controls

Project Background

About Controlco

Controlco brings together building systems and networks through a web-based IoT app that lets users control their facility operations. The app consists of data visualizations and HVAC settings that are automated to maintain favorable temperatures and conditions in commercial buildings.

The Problem

User-centered design wasn't practiced at Controlco, and as a result, features were being added without truly empathizing with users and understanding their day-to-day workflow. After receiving enough feedback from users about how "primitive" our app was, the VP of Product made a commitment to improve our design and overall experience for users.

The old Controlco app which was built without any UX designers

Role and Collaboration

As the sole UX designer in the company, I led the redesign efforts with a team consisting of a product manager, front-end engineer, and systems engineer. I handled the redesign from end to end, reaching out to users, producing wireframes, coming up with the visual design, delivered the final prototypes, and tested the usability.

Learning About the User

Who Are the Users of the Controlco App?

The power users are field/ops technicians who are responsible for maintaining the HVAC conditions within commercial buildings. The type of people in such positions are mostly males between their 30 - 60s and come from blue collar backgrounds. From working closely with them, I've learned that most of our users are limited in their knowledge of the latest trends in modern technologies.

Persona: The Field Technician

James Fleck

James logs into the app daily to make sure that the equipment is running as intended. He creates schedules and changes settings in certain air handler units. User Research

I reached out to two users from the shared workspace company that we were building for. Through RingCentral video calls, the PM and I were able to observe how they were using the Controlco application.

As users were talking me through what they do on the app, they didn't hold back any opinions. The first screen they see is supposedly the "homescreen", but is actually a duplicate of the HVAC screen. Users complained about not having a high-level view of their entire system's health, so they would click page by page to see certain statuses of each individual unit. They also pointed out that creating a schedule was tedious because there were extraneous actions involved for something so simple like creating schedules.

Inconsistent type and colors were being used without any reason

Incorrect implementation of modals and dropdowns

"I Hope You Don't Change Too Much of the App"

The app definitely looks bad and is pretty clunky, but I'm used to it now. I don't want to spend a lot of time relearning everything.
- An ops technician

While users called out the product's bad visual design choices and clunky experience, they also made it clear that they did not want something drastically different and "flashy". Learnability was a concern for them because they are not a technical audience. My response was that the redesign would simplify the processes and reduce the effort needed to get their jobs done. I was able to earn their trust in letting them know that the redesign would make simple tasks way easier to do.

Requirements and Planning

Users Want Simplicity and Efficiency

After conducting the research, I synced with the PM to finalize what the enhancements should be in order to help users. Our insights led us to these user goals:

  1. Create schedules quickly
  2. Change facility settings more easily

Users are able to complete their day to day tasks, but not without frustration. The unusual interaction patterns on our app added up to make the experience more difficult than it should be to carry out simple tasks (like the dropdowns).

The Solution: Homepage With KPIs and Platform-wide Facelift

In order to help users, the biggest action item we proposed was to design a homepage that shows all the high-level data points. What we didn't want to do was remove all things familiar to the user. Some pages would require a facelift, while others would require a complete redesign. I went through the existing app and audited interaction patterns that need improvements.

The Design Process

Mocking up the New Homepage

I created a low fideilty wireframe of the homepage to show how many units are offline/online/faulty. There were other important KPIs like temperature and humidity which users specifically asked to see. This allows users to see the most useful bits of information immediately without having to sift through all devices. Users can also click on the KPIs to see a modal that shows other details about the units.

The homepage which would autopopulate the data points for any facility

Enhancing the Schedule Creation Flow

The enhanced schedule creation screens

Some interaction patterns have been changed on the Schedule screen so that there was better information architecture, surfacing the most important items. For example, dropdowns for times were changed into toggles instead.

Adding in Central Plant Data

I met with the engineers and PM to talk about the wireframes to ensure that the design was within reason given the timeline and bandwidth. There were no challenges on either end, but I found out from the PM that this client is integrating another facility that has a central plant. We would need to redesign the homepage to scale and reflect other properties that contain such systems. I received input from the PM about what data points they would want to see regarding the central plant and made the changes in my designs.

Version 2: backend would detect those data of central plant and auto-populate on the UI

Visual Designs

Since we didn't have a design standard in place, I explored some options for our color palette and type. I had some proposals from the VP, saying specifically that the app should be a lighter interface since many of our other products use dark blues. We couldn't think of a scenario where that would be an issue, so we ran with it. I was careful to make sure that none of the colors would coincide with the current colors we were using for statuses, and that everything had enough contrast to be readable.

We tested several typefaces, but ended up using Avenir Medium for body content because of its scalability on the mobile version. Avenir Condensed was used for styling our labels.

Evaluating the Usability

Testing out the Usability of the Upgraded Platform

After we reviewed the build internally with no issues, we rolled it out for the customer of one site. To test out the workflow of the most common user scenarios, I ran a few guerrilla-styled usability tests with some of the technicians. I recruited six participants and had three of them perform tasks on the old interface, while the three participants performed the same tasks using the new design.

The users were to do the following:

  1. Report how many units are occupied.
  2. Add the following event to August 1, 2017: Event named "cats", event class "Override", start time is "this time", end time mode is "sunrise", and end time is "2:30pm", end date is "August 9, 2017."

For users using the legacy system, the average time it took to complete the two tasks, respectively, were 2.44 seconds and 25.32 seconds. For the new interface, users completed the tasks in 1.02 seconds and 20.21 seconds. Although by a small margin, users completed their common tasks faster with the newly redesigned interface.

Process Overview

Define the Problem

How can we improve the workflow for our users so they can do their jobs with optimal satisfaction and with the least amount of time?

Gather Information

Interviewed users to learn about journey and pain points

Ideate

Brainstormed idea for homepage/dashboard

Reiterate

Learned about facility's integration of central plant and redesigned homepage to reflect that

Visual Design

Focused on aesthetics to create delightful experience

Implement

Took about two weeks of UX effort (ideation, user interviews, prototyping, reiteration) and one month for engineering to build out

Evaluate

Ran usability test with six customers and concluded that new interface outperformed the old

The Outcome and Impact

A New Look for the Controlco Platform

The new interface is Apple-y. It is pretty clean and simple to use. Reminds me of this iPad game that my daughter plays.
- An ops technician

The final product was warmly received by all stakeholders (VP, engineering, data modelers, and users). People said that it was much easier to use, and that the redesign made the app much more "pleasant" to deal with. One data modeler even said that the new interface was "Apple-like" and reminded him of his daughter's iPad game. The customers ended up implementing this design across all of their enterpirse level projects in the portfolio.

The Takeaway: Thoughts and Lessons

I felt that Controlco made an earnest effort to start applying user-centered design approaches and testing methods by allowing me to take the project from end to end. The VP regarded it as a shift towards a new paradigm of Controlco's products. If given another shot to revisit the product with more resources, I would have focused on the microinteractions involving the HVAC pages. There were so many things that could have been improved, but we were slammed with different priorities and didn't have the resources to completely overhaul major chunks of the platform.
