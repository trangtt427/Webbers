About

With 8 years of experience designing across a colorful spectrum of industries, products, and audiences, I enjoy coming up with solutions that put the spotlight on people.

My path to product design wasn't a straight shot—I studied biology and started off working in medical. In my soul-searching, I rekindled what was tucked away in the depths of my brain during my academic adult era: my knack for creativity and ingenuity. I leveraged my eye for aesthetics and visual design fundamentals that I still retained over the years, familiarized myself with human-centered design, taught myself the tools of the trade, and at last found my way into my first design role creating real value for others at the intersection of my passions. This was where I earned my stripes; I had found my calling designing digital products.

Since then, I have worked at startups and collaborated with product development teams through uncharted and complex territories to come up with timely solutions that are purposeful, grounded in research, and pleasantly simple to use. I'm an all-around designer with a sweet spot for finding touchpoints that are ripe for connecting business needs to user value. From nurturing budding ideas into solid product strategies backed by company objectives, to imbuing even the smallest user interactions with delight, I look for ways to make impact by creating experiences that center around people.

Core Principles

Always consider the audience

For me, being a designer goes beyond how we show up in our work—it means being conscious of every interaction we have with others, considering how they feel, and not assuming that others know what we're thinking about right off the bat. We should be mindful in the ways we communicate in order to bring people onto the same wavelength.

Mind the details

Whatever I put out in the world, I aim to do it thoughtfully and executed at the highest caliber of design. Every bit of detail has meaning and the smallest design choice can liven up people in positive ways.

Embrace humor

I take my work seriously but I don't take myself seriously. I crack jokes; I communicate through memes and GIFs; I've learned to roll with the punches and put things in a lighthearted perspective. Laughing and having fun while building products are underrated.
