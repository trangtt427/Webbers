About

With 8 years of experience designing across a colorful spectrum of industries, products, and audiences, I enjoy coming up with solutions that put the spotlight on people.

An aspiring medical professional turned product designer—I welcome big challenges. I was introduced to human-centered design and its impact on products and people, taught myself the tools of the trade, and landed my first design job making software usable for a non-technical audience. From nurturing budding ideas into north star visions to sprinkling delight into the smallest moments of user interactions, I look for ways to create positive impact at any chance I can get.

Core Principles

Always consider the audience

Designing products is just one of the many ways I demonstrate my consideration for others. I'm at peak awareness and empathy whenever I show up in front of others.

Mind the details

I design with the highest level of thoughtfulness because even the most unassuming design choice has potential to stir up something positive for users.

Embrace humor

I take my work seriously but I don't take myself seriously. Even when things don't go according to plan, I try to inject some lightheartedness and humor. Laughing and having fun with others while building products is totally underrated.
