The Challenge

Designing for an ABM Start‑up

At Engagio, I supported all marketing campaigns with visual assets for both web and print, including redesigning our corporate website and landing pages to improve the user experience.

My goal was to promote engagement through stimulating visuals and pleasant interactions on our channels.

I was the brand guardian hired to maintain design consistency and help Engagio move away from their old looks and towards a more modern aesthetic.

Demand Gen Assets

Social and Email Banners

Most of my ongoing work involved with the creation of graphics to promote our campaigns through all channels of acquisition. I've contributed to a design system which includes templates and assets that are reusable for all promotions.

Before my arrival, the company was using a lot of nautical stock photos, turquoise overlays, and a whale character by the name of Gio. While Gio was regarded as a lovable mascot within our company, we felt that it was important to limit him on our consumer-facing items because we wanted to maintain a serious identity as an ABM company.

Brochures and Print

Website and Landing Pages

The Clear and Complete Guide to ABM Analytics

During periods of promotional campaigns, I would create accompanying graphics and homepage highlights, and build out landing pages to be implemented into Marketo. One of the biggest things we promoted was the Clear and Complete Guide to ABM Analytics, written by our CEO Jon Miller.

ABM Next Level Experience

An Engagio Event

Every year, Engagio hosts their own marketing event around the same time as Marketo Summit. My role during this time was to design for the entire campaign and event, including all demand gen assets and conference collateral. I worked closely with the Field Events Marketer and the Product Marketer to forge the identity and messaging involved.

ABM Next Level Experience was held at Jillian's in San Francisco

The Takeaway

From Maritime to Modern

During my time at Engagio, I was able to leave an imprint on the company. The Marketing Team has adopted some of my design standards and guidelines. We were able to move away from the excess use of nautical imagery but still kept our playful mascot Gio close by when appropriate.

Besides leaving behind something for the company, I was also able to take away a lot from working with the team there. I had to figure out crafty ways to communicate to a less technical crowd, while working with sporadic deadlines. In the end, the skills acquired here set me up to work in my subsequent positions in Marketing.
